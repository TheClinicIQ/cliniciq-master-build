## Sections to Produce

### Section 1 — The 5 Video Formats

Document each format completely: what it is, when to use it, where it appears in the funnel, production approach, and the specific production standard.

**Format 1 — VSL (Video Sales Letter)**
Length: 15–45 minutes
Audience: cold to warm traffic
Delivery: funnel page, webinar replay page
Production approach: talking head + b-roll, or expert-narrated slide presentation, or hybrid
Copy architecture: Perfect Webinar (Brunson) or Fladlein framework — follows step13b_webinar_builder_deep_spec.md
Critical production truth: audio quality over visual quality. A mediocre-looking video with great audio and great copy converts better than a beautiful video with mediocre audio. The voice is the primary trust signal.
When VSL over written sales page: when the offer is complex, when the mechanism requires explanation, when the expert's personality is central to the trust equation.

**Format 2 — Short-Form Ad (15–60 seconds)**
Length: 15–60 seconds
Audience: cold traffic acquisition
Delivery: Meta Feed, Stories, Instagram Reels, TikTok
Production approach: single continuous take OR rapid cut sequence, direct-to-camera or voice-over-b-roll
Purpose: drive one action only — webinar registration, lead magnet opt-in, or direct offer for lower-priced products
The non-negotiable: hook in frames 0–3. Everything else can vary. This cannot.

**Format 3 — Medium-Form Ad (60–180 seconds)**
Length: 60–180 seconds
Audience: warm retargeting — people who have visited, opted in, or engaged
Delivery: Meta Feed, YouTube pre-roll, Instagram
Production approach: more story, more proof, more mechanism depth than short-form
Purpose: advance belief for people already in the funnel — build on existing awareness

**Format 4 — UGC-Style (30–90 seconds)**
Length: 30–90 seconds
Audience: cold to warm
Delivery: Meta Feed, Instagram Reels, TikTok
Production approach: casual direct-to-camera, phone aesthetic, natural setting, no visible production equipment
Types:
- Expert-as-UGC: the health expert in a personal setting speaking directly and informally
- Patient/Character UGC: first-person transformation story in the patient's voice — real patient, actor, or AI avatar
The authenticity standard: must feel like something a real person shared organically, not something an advertising team produced.

**Format 5 — B-Roll Cinematic (15–90 seconds)**
Length: variable
Audience: any
Delivery: used as a layer, rarely standalone
Production approach: no talking head — pure visual storytelling with music and/or voice-over
Purpose: mechanism visualization, lifestyle aspiration, brand awareness, supporting layer that elevates other formats

### Section 2 — The Hook Sequence: Frames 0:00–0:03

The most important section in this document. The hook sequence is inviolable — no exceptions, no format exemptions.

**The "Break Glass in Frame 1" Rule**
Whatever would most likely cause the right person to stop their scroll appears in frame 1. Not the second most compelling thing. The most compelling thing available. The viewer's scroll decision is made within the first second. Frame 1 is the only frame that matters for acquisition.

**The Three Elements of a Video Hook (all three present by frame 3):**

1. Visual hook — one dominant element creating pattern interrupt: face with strong emotional expression (curiosity, surprise, concern, or relief — never neutral), OR high-contrast bold text card, OR unexpected scene creating immediate curiosity. One element dominant — never two competing.

2. Audio hook — the first spoken words are the hook. Not "Hey guys." Not "In today's video." Not the expert's name. The hook is the first word spoken. Options: provocative question, surprising statement, bold claim, counterintuitive revelation.

3. Text overlay hook — hook appears as text on screen within the first 5 frames. Text and spoken word aligned — the same hook expressed in both channels simultaneously.

**The alignment rule:** Visual hook + audio hook + text overlay must all reinforce the same message. Three-channel confirmation is what makes the viewer stop. Any misalignment between channels reduces the stop power.

**What destroys hook effectiveness:**
- Any introduction before the hook (name, greeting, "in this video")
- Logo animation or brand opener
- Music fade-in without simultaneous visual hook
- Generic opening visuals (sitting down, adjusting camera, looking off-screen)
- Text appearing after a delay on a text-first hook
- Neutral or professional expression on a face hook — no emotion = no stop power

**20 Fully Written Video Hook Examples for Health Content:**

*Curiosity Gap hooks:*
1. "The reason your thyroid medication isn't working has nothing to do with your thyroid." (visual: close on speaker's face, expression: controlled revelation)
2. "I've seen this pattern in over 3,000 patients with hormone problems. Nobody talks about it." (visual: direct to camera, expression: slight gravity)
3. "What your doctor isn't telling you about your lab results — and it's not what you think." (visual: speaker leaning slightly forward, expression: urgency)
4. "Every single patient I've ever helped reverse this condition had one thing in common. Here's what it was." (visual: direct camera, text overlay: "one thing in common")
5. "There are two types of [condition]. One gets better. One gets worse. Here's how to tell which one you have." (visual: split frame or direct camera)

*Reversal/Counterintuitive hooks:*
6. "Stop doing everything your doctor told you to do for [condition]. Seriously." (visual: direct camera, slightly challenging expression)
7. "The healthier you try to eat, the worse your gut feels? There's a reason for that." (visual: direct camera with genuine concern expression)
8. "I used to think [old approach] was the answer. I was treating thousands of patients wrong." (visual: honest, slightly vulnerable expression)
9. "Your stress isn't a willpower problem. It's a physiology problem. And here's the difference." (visual: clear, authoritative expression)
10. "Waking up tired after 8 hours of sleep isn't a sleep problem. Watch this." (visual: direct camera, text overlay: "not a sleep problem")

*Empathy Mirror hooks:*
11. "If you've been told your labs are normal but you feel anything but — this is for you." (visual: warm, genuine eye contact)
12. "The 2pm crash. The brain fog. The weight that won't move no matter what you try. I see you." (visual: warm, recognition expression)
13. "You've done everything right. Clean eating. Exercise. Good sleep. And you still feel terrible. Here's why." (visual: genuine empathy)
14. "If your doctor has ever said 'everything looks fine' and you've gone home feeling more alone than when you went in — stay with me." (visual: quiet, direct)
15. "Three years of trying to fix this. Four doctors. No answers. Sound familiar?" (visual: recognition in expression)

*Authority Reveal hooks:*
16. "I've helped [number] patients reverse [condition]. The pattern is always the same thing." (visual: confidence without arrogance)
17. "In 15 years of functional medicine I've never seen [standard approach] actually fix this root cause. Here's what does." (visual: authoritative, direct)
18. "My clinic sees [number] new patients a month with [condition]. Every single one has this in common." (visual: clinical authority, warm delivery)

*Transformation Proof hooks:*
19. "[Patient first name] came to me [specific symptom]. [Specific timeframe] later, here's what happened." (visual: cut to patient photo or direct-to-camera speaking)
20. "She was on [medication] for 11 years. Here's what changed in 90 days when we stopped chasing the symptom." (visual: direct camera, text overlay: "11 years → 90 days")

### Section 3 — Short-Form Structure (15–60 Seconds): Second by Second

**0–3 seconds: The Hook**
As in Section 2. Visual + audio + text aligned. The right person stops here.

**3–8 seconds: The Bridge**
One of three functions:
- Agitation bridge: extend the pain — "And the worst part is, most [practitioners] are still treating the symptom instead of the root cause."
- Credibility bridge: rapid proof — "I've helped [number] patients reverse [condition] and the pattern is always the same."
- Curiosity bridge: open the loop wider — "What I'm about to show you is something most people with [condition] have never been told."

**8–20 seconds: The Substance**
One specific thing — not a list. Either the root cause mechanism named and briefly explained, OR one specific patient result with enough detail to be credible. Specificity rule: "Hashimoto's" not "thyroid issues." "93 days" not "a few months." "She reduced her medication dose by 60%" not "she improved dramatically."

**20–50 seconds: The Build** (60-second format only)
Extend the mechanism (a second insight that makes the first more powerful) OR add social proof (a second patient story or a number that contextualizes the result). For 15–30 second format: skip to CTA.

**50–60 seconds: The CTA**
One action only. Specific. The viewer knows exactly what happens when they click. "Register for the free training at [URL]" / "Grab the free guide — link in bio" / "Book your free assessment — [URL]." Never "check out my website" or "learn more."

**Compression rules:**
- 60 seconds → 30 seconds: compress or remove the Build section; keep Hook, Bridge, Substance, CTA
- 30 seconds → 15 seconds: compress Bridge into Hook (make the hook also the bridge), keep Substance brief, keep CTA

### Section 4 — VSL Structure from a Production Standpoint

The agent has read `step13b_webinar_builder_deep_spec.md`. This section documents production requirements per VSL phase — the visual and audio decisions that make the copy architecture work on camera.

**Phase 1 — Introduction (10–15 min)**
Production: talking head, medium close-up framing (chest to top of head with room above). Expression must match content: authority establishment = confident, calm; hope installation = warm, genuine; open loops = slight forward lean, slightly heightened energy. Text overlays for specific promises. No b-roll in introduction — the expert's face is establishing trust. Interrupt with b-roll only for a credibility visual (patient photo, clinic environment).

**Phase 2 — The Three Secrets (35–45 min)**
Production: "story face" vs. "teaching face" — Stories need close framing with emotional expression matching the narrative. Teaching needs slightly wider frame with visual support for mechanisms (diagram, patient photo, relevant b-roll at the transition points). Cut to b-roll during mechanism teaching, return to talking head for the emotional beats of each Secret.

**Phase 3 — The Stack and Close (25–35 min)**
Production: the stack slide must be visible and readable. If the expert presents the stack on camera, show the slide on screen with the expert's face in a corner PiP (picture-in-picture). The price reveal is a direct-to-camera moment — no looking away, no cutting to slides at the exact moment of price announcement. The CTA URL appears on screen and stays visible. The guarantee section: trust visual on screen (shield, checkmark) plus direct-to-camera delivery of the terms with genuine conviction.

**Audio non-negotiable:** Peaks at -12dB to -6dB. Noise floor at -60dB or lower. Consistent EQ with warm mid-range presence. Level for mobile listening without headphones at 50% volume — this is the median viewing environment.

**Full (75–90 min) vs. Short (45–60 min):**
What compresses: the introduction (5–8 min from 10–15), Secrets 2 and 3 (combined section with one Epiphany Bridge covering both), the b-roll density in the teaching sections.
What never compresses: the complete stack and close sequence (all 12 steps in order), the trial close frequency (every 60 seconds throughout), the price reveal and guarantee sections.

### Section 5 — UGC-Style Production Standard

**Expert-as-UGC:**
- Setting: personal — home office, kitchen, clinic hallway, outdoors. Never a professional filming set.
- Lighting: natural window light or single ring light. Not three-point professional lighting.
- Camera: phone camera preferred or DSLR set to look like phone camera. Portrait orientation for Stories/Reels.
- Delivery: conversational, direct-to-camera, no teleprompter feel. Natural speech patterns including brief pauses and self-corrections are authenticity signals.
- The authenticity test: if this appeared in someone's social feed without an advertising label, would it look like a real person sharing genuine knowledge? If yes — correct format. If it looks like advertising — the UGC aesthetic has failed.

**Patient/Character UGC — Three Production Paths:**
1. Real patient filmed on their own device — the gold standard. Maximum authenticity.
2. Actor filmed in UGC style playing the patient character from the dossier — requires careful direction to prevent the "acting" quality from showing.
3. AI avatar in UGC aesthetic — for scale. Use most advanced avatars only. If the voice sounds synthetic to an untrained ear, it is not ready for use.

**Story arc for all three:** before → discovery → transformation → result. Specific details at every stage. The specificity test: real stories have specific, specific, specific details. Scripted stories have general, general, general details. "I had been to four different doctors in three years and every single one told me my labs were normal" is specific and credible. "I struggled with health problems for a long time" is neither.

### Section 6 — Text Overlay System

**Placement:** Center-frame safe zone only — not top 14% (platform UI: handles, time) or bottom 20% (platform UI: CTA buttons, captions). For talking head: text in lower-center or upper-center, never overlapping speaker's face.

**Timing:** Text appears on the word it emphasizes. Text disappears or transitions before 3–4 seconds. The eye reads in 2 seconds — text that stays longer creates lag.

**Line length:** Maximum 6 words per card. More than 6 words: split across two sequential cards.

**Contrast treatment (in order of reliability):**
1. Text on solid color band
2. Text with 2px+ outline in contrasting color
3. Text with drop shadow
4. Semi-transparent overlay behind text

**Font weight:** Bold or heavy only. Thin or regular weight text at video resolution is illegible at small viewing sizes.

**Color:** White text with black outline is the universal reliable standard. Brand color text requires contrast verification against every background it may appear over.

### Section 7 — B-Roll Selection and Direction

**The Specific Over Generic Mandate**
The single most important b-roll principle.

| Generic (don't use) | Specific (use instead) |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| "A person jogging" | Person running a specific trail, breath visible in morning light |
| "A salad" | Colorful vegetables being cut on a wooden board, knife work visible |
| "Doctor and patient talking" | Doctor leaning slightly forward in genuine engagement, patient's expression shifting to understanding |
| "Healthy morning routine" | Specific morning ritual: specific beverage preparation, specific light quality, specific sequence |
| "The gut" | Medical illustration of the gut-brain axis, or food fermenting in close-up |

**Shot types for health content:**

Movement/vitality: outdoor activity, cooking with energy, playing with children, morning routines that signal aliveness and presence. The emotion shown: vitality, presence, engagement. Not performance, not perfection — presence.

Food/nutrition: preparation over finished product. The sensory experience: chopping, stirring, the color of whole ingredients. Natural kitchen, natural light.

Clinical-adjacent: the expert in their element with warmth. The moment of connection. The turn in a patient's expression when they understand something for the first time. These are the highest-value b-roll shots in health video — they show the transformation happening.

**The contrast principle:** Cut from talking head to relevant b-roll every 20–30 seconds in VSL/medium-form. B-roll reinforces the emotional state being built — when naming the mechanism, show its context; when describing the patient experience, show the experience.

### Section 8 — Audio Standards

**Voice track:**
- Peaks: -12dB to -6dB
- Noise floor: -60dB or lower
- EQ: warm mid-range presence, no harsh high-frequency content, no room reverb
- Compression: light-to-moderate for consistent volume
- Monitor target: sounds good on phone speaker at 50% volume without headphones

**Music bed:**
- VSL: present but never dominant. Volume: 20–30% of voice track. Fades to near-silence at emotional revelation moments (the epiphany, the mechanism reveal, the price reveal). Fades back in at transitions.
- Short-form: music from frame 1, voice on top, music at 30–40% of voice volume. Music drives the energy urgency.
- Hook moment: music drop to near-silence at the revelation is more powerful than any music swell. Silence creates emphasis.

**AI voice standard (when used):**
- Natural cadence with pauses at punctuation — no robotic continuous delivery
- Emotional variation matching the copy: warmth in patient stories, authority in mechanism sections, conviction in the close
- Not ready to use if: synthetic quality is detectable to an untrained ear, pauses are mechanical rather than natural, emotional register is flat throughout

### Section 9 — Platform Delivery

Cross-reference `platform_dimensions.md` for technical specs. This section governs creative standards.

**Meta Feed:** Premium but native-feeling. Sound-off consideration — text overlay must carry the hook even without audio (40% of Meta video watched without sound). Captions/subtitles required for all content over 15 seconds.

**Instagram Reels and TikTok:** Authentic > polished. The UGC aesthetic outperforms produced in this format. Hook even more critical — swipe velocity is higher. Caption/text overlay more important for sound-off viewing.

**YouTube pre-roll:** First 5 seconds must contain the hook AND a reason not to skip. The "don't skip" reason is distinct from the hook — a specific promise that the next 30 seconds delivers more value than the content the viewer was about to watch.

**VSL on funnel page:** Professional, not corporate. Thumbnail follows YouTube thumbnail formula. Autoplay without sound (browser policy) — first visible frame must do the hook job without audio. Opt-in for sound, not required.
