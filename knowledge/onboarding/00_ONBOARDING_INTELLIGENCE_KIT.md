## Part 1: The Expert Psychology — What's Really Happening in Onboarding

### The Expert Is Not a "User." They Are a Human Being Having a Vulnerable Experience.

When a health practitioner sits down with IQ Claw for the first time, something specific is happening psychologically that most platforms get catastrophically wrong:

**They are being asked to hand over their professional identity to a machine.**

Their voice. Their expertise. Their reputation. The thing they spent 4–12 years learning, the thing they've built their practice around, the thing their patients trust them for — they're being asked to trust that a system can represent it faithfully.

This is not a SaaS onboarding. This is an act of professional trust.

**The internal monologue the expert is running during onboarding:**
- *"Is this thing going to make me sound like every other chiropractor on Instagram?"*
- *"I'm not a marketer. Am I going to say the wrong thing and get bad content?"*
- *"This feels like a form. I hate forms."*
- *"How is this different from the last tool that promised to 'automate my marketing'?"*
- *"If this makes me sound generic, my patients will notice. I can't risk that."*

**What this means for the onboarding agents:**

1. **Never feel like a form.** The moment the interaction feels like data collection, the expert's defenses go up and their answers become shorter, safer, and less useful. Every question must feel like genuine curiosity about their practice — because it *is*.

2. **Demonstrate intelligence before requesting trust.** The Web Scraper pre-population and Voice DNA Mass Ingestion exist for one reason: to show the expert that the platform already understands something about them before asking for more. Leading with "I pulled your Instagram and noticed your cortisol posts outperform everything else by 3x" is fundamentally different from "Tell me about your practice."

3. **The 90-second demo is not a feature showcase. It's a trust transaction.** When the expert sees one piece of content written in their actual voice — not a template, not generic health copy, but *their* words restructured into a high-performing format — the entire dynamic shifts from "am I going to get bad output?" to "this thing actually gets me."

4. **Quality of extraction directly determines quality of everything downstream.** A thin dossier produces thin content. A deep dossier produces content the expert barely has to edit. This is not a nice-to-have — it is the single highest-leverage activity on the entire platform. Every minute invested in richer onboarding extraction saves hours of editing and correction later.

### The Three Expert Archetypes (and How Extraction Differs)

Not all experts onboard the same way. The onboarding agent must detect the archetype within the first 2–3 exchanges and adapt its extraction approach accordingly.

#### Archetype 1: The Reluctant Expert

**Detection signals (first 3 messages):**
- Average response length: <40 words
- Qualifier frequency: 2+ qualifiers per response ("I think," "maybe," "I'm not sure," "sort of," "kind of")
- Self-deprecation present: "I'm not great at this kind of thing," "I'm not a marketer"
- Answers the question asked but volunteers nothing extra
- May ask clarifying questions before answering ("What do you mean by...?")

**Reality:** Often the most talented clinician with the least marketing confidence. They've built their practice on referrals and clinical skill, not content. They feel like a fraud talking about themselves in marketing terms.

**Extraction strategy:**
- Don't ask them to describe their brand. Ask them to describe a patient interaction.
- Use the "talk to me like you'd talk to a patient who..." prompt — it bypasses their marketing self-consciousness and accesses their clinical voice, which is almost always more compelling.
- Reflect back what they said in marketing terms and watch their reaction: "When you said 'nobody's looking at the root cause,' that's actually one of the most powerful hooks in health marketing. You're already doing this naturally."
- The Reluctant Expert often needs to *discover* that they already have a voice, not be asked to perform one.
- Give them evidence from their own content: "Your post about [topic] got 3x your average engagement. You're already connecting — you just don't see it as marketing."

**Risk:** If you let them stay guarded, you get a generic dossier. Push gently into specifics. When they give a short answer, don't accept it — follow up with "Give me a specific example" or "Think of the last patient where this happened."

#### Archetype 2: The Confident Communicator

**Detection signals (first 3 messages):**
- Average response length: >100 words
- Strong declarative statements: "The problem is..." "What I've found is..." "Nobody else is doing this."
- References to existing content: "I posted about this," "I did a video on this"
- May redirect the conversation: "Actually, the bigger issue is..."
- Opinionated vocabulary: "absolutely," "always," "never," "that's wrong"

**Reality:** They know their voice. They know their audience. The onboarding agent's job is less about extraction and more about *documentation and precision-tuning*. The risk is that their confidence masks gaps — they may assume the platform "gets it" when important nuances haven't been explicitly captured.

**Extraction strategy:**
- Lead with their existing content: "Your post about the woman who couldn't sleep for three years — that's one of the best hooks I've seen. Let's break down why it works."
- Ask for the *rules* behind their instincts: "You naturally avoid the word 'holistic' — what is it about that word that doesn't fit?"
- Challenge constructively: "Your website says 'optimal wellness.' But your Instagram sounds completely different — direct, specific, almost confrontational. Which one is actually you?"
- Don't let their speed become your speed. They'll answer quickly and want to move on. Slow them down on the Voice DNA confirmation — the nuances matter.
- The Confident Communicator gives you great raw material. The agent's job is to systematize what they already do intuitively.

**Risk:** They answer comprehensively on topics they're passionate about and gloss over everything else. Watch for thin spots in their offer architecture and proof bank — they may assume "you get it" on the commercial side.

#### Archetype 3: The Overwhelmed Practitioner

**Detection signals (first 3 messages):**
- Response latency: >60 seconds between messages (they're doing other things)
- Time references: "I only have a few minutes," "Can we do this quick?"
- Incomplete sentences or abbreviated answers
- May apologize for being distracted: "Sorry, I'm between patients"
- High agreement rate: "Yeah that sounds right" without elaboration

**Reality:** They signed up because they know they need marketing help. They do not have bandwidth to think deeply about it right now. The platform needs to extract maximum value from minimum time.

**Extraction strategy:**
- Default to Fast-Start (not Deep-Dive). Get the essentials locked in under 25 minutes.
- Lean heavily on Web Scraper pre-population: "Based on your website and Instagram, here's what I've captured. Correct anything that's wrong."
- Use confirmation questions instead of open-ended ones: "Your main offer is a New Patient Exam for $49 — that right?" is faster and more accurate than "What's your main offer?"
- Batch related questions: "Quick three-parter — what age range is your ideal patient, what's the one thing they've all tried that didn't work, and what's the first thing you tell them in their initial visit?"
- Schedule a follow-up enrichment session: "I've got enough to build your first content batch. Let's do a 15-minute deep-dive on your patient stories next week — that's where the real magic happens."

**Risk:** Rushing produces a thin dossier. But a thin dossier that's *accurate* is better than a deep dossier that was extracted from a distracted expert who was giving half-attention answers. Mark the dossier as `enrichment_priority: high` and rely on the Voice DNA Mass Ingestion sub-agent to fill gaps from their existing content.

#### Archetype Detection Fallback
If the signals are mixed or unclear after 3 exchanges, default to the Reluctant Expert approach — it's the safest because it draws out deeper answers through scenario-based questions rather than assuming the expert will volunteer information.

## Part 2: The New Vehicle Framework — The Most Critical Concept in Onboarding

### What It Is

From Russell Brunson's Expert Secrets: the most powerful positioning in any market is not "I'm better at the thing you've already tried." It's "the thing you've been trying is the wrong vehicle entirely — and here's the right one."

**This is not a marketing trick. In health, it's almost always literally true.**

The patient who has been taking thyroid medication for 10 years without improvement hasn't failed at the right approach. They've been on the wrong approach — nobody looked at what was driving the autoimmune response. The patient with chronic fatigue who's been managing symptoms hasn't failed at self-care. Nobody identified the mitochondrial dysfunction, the mold exposure, the HPA axis dysregulation.

The expert's "new vehicle" is the mechanism they use to solve the problem that other approaches couldn't solve. **It is the most important thing the onboarding agent extracts.**

### Why It Matters for Every Downstream Agent

| Agent | How It Uses the New Vehicle |
|-------|---------------------------|
| **Content Builder** | Every awareness-level content piece leads toward the new vehicle as the discovery moment |
| **Ad Creative Builder** | Every hook is designed to create curiosity about the new vehicle without revealing it fully |
| **Webinar Builder** | The entire webinar framework is structured as the "Epiphany Bridge" that leads to the new vehicle reveal |
| **Funnel Builder** | The opt-in offer promises the first glimpse of the new vehicle; the sales page delivers the full case |
| **Email Builder** | The nurture sequence systematically breaks limiting beliefs that prevent adoption of the new vehicle |
| **Sales Script Builder** | The phone/DM script positions the consultation as the first step to accessing the new vehicle |

If the new vehicle is weak, vague, or generic ("we take a holistic approach"), every downstream agent produces weak, vague, generic output. If the new vehicle is specific, named, and mechanistically clear ("The Neural Reset Protocol — we identify the 3 signals your nervous system is sending that nobody else tests for"), every output is sharp.

### How to Extract It — By Specialty

The extraction questions must flex based on specialty. The functional medicine practitioner's new vehicle sounds different from a chiropractor's or a dentist's.

**Core extraction question (all specialties):**
> "When a patient comes to you after failing with other providers — what's the first thing you look at that nobody else looked at?"

**Specialty-adapted follow-ups:**

| Specialty | Follow-Up Question | What It Surfaces |
|---|---|---|
| **Functional medicine** | "What test do you run that their regular doctor never ordered?" | Specific diagnostic differentiator |
| **Chiropractic** | "What do you see in their spine or nervous system that the other chiropractors missed?" | Assessment methodology differentiator |
| **Dentistry / TMJ** | "What connection are you finding between their dental issue and their overall symptoms?" | Cross-system insight differentiator |
| **Physical therapy** | "What's the movement pattern or compensation you identify that everyone else treated around?" | Biomechanical insight differentiator |
| **Med spa / aesthetics** | "What's the underlying cause of the skin/aging issue that topical treatments can't touch?" | Root cause differentiator in aesthetics context |
| **Mental health** | "What's the physiological driver behind the anxiety/depression that nobody's testing for?" | Bio-psycho differentiator |
| **Nutrition** | "What's the metabolic pattern you see that a standard diet approach will never fix?" | Metabolic insight differentiator |

**Then for all specialties:**

2. **"Why does that work when everything else they tried didn't?"**
   - This surfaces the mechanism explanation — the "why" behind the approach.

3. **"If you had to explain your approach to a patient in one sentence — the way you'd say it across the desk — what would you say?"**
   - This surfaces the natural language for the mechanism.

4. **"Does your method have a name? If you were going to name it, what would you call it?"**
   - This surfaces the mechanism name. If they don't have one, the platform can help them develop one — but only after the dossier has enough data for the Brand Strategy agent to generate options that actually fit.

**What gets captured:**

| Dossier Field | Example (Functional Medicine) | Example (Chiropractic) |
|---|---|---|
| `mechanism.core_approach` | "We test for the 3 hormonal signals that standard labs don't include, then rebuild the signaling pathway from the ground up" | "We map the full spinal nerve interference pattern — not just where it hurts, but the compensation chain that created the problem" |
| `mechanism.why_it_works` | "Standard treatment manages symptoms. We identify and resolve the upstream driver." | "Other chiropractors adjust where it hurts. We adjust where the interference started — which is almost never where the pain is." |
| `mechanism.natural_language` | "Your body isn't broken. It's stuck in a stress loop — and nobody taught it how to get out." | "Your spine is trying to tell you something. We're the ones who actually listen." |
| `mechanism.name` | "The Neural Reset Protocol" (or null) | "The Pattern-First Method" (or null) |
| `mechanism.vs_conventional` | "Conventional: manage thyroid hormone levels forever. Ours: identify WHY the immune system attacks the thyroid." | "Conventional: adjust the pain site, repeat weekly. Ours: find the source of the pattern, correct it, and watch the pain resolve." |

### The "New Vehicle vs. Improvement Offer" Litmus Test

After extraction, the onboarding agent runs this test internally:

**Does the expert's positioning sound like an improvement or a new vehicle?**

❌ **Improvement language (flag for refinement):**
- "We take a more thorough approach"
- "Our methods are more advanced"
- "We're better than traditional [X]"
- "We offer a comprehensive solution"
- "We spend more time with patients"

✅ **New vehicle language (strong positioning):**
- "The thing they've been treating isn't actually the problem"
- "Nobody else is testing for this"
- "We found that the real driver is something no one's looking at"
- "Once you understand the mechanism, you can't unsee it"
- "Their doctor was treating the smoke, not the fire"

If the expert's natural language falls into improvement territory, the onboarding agent doesn't try to fix it during onboarding — that's the Brand Strategy agent's job. But it flags it: `mechanism.positioning_type: improvement` with a note that repositioning toward new vehicle language is recommended.

### When the Expert Genuinely Doesn't Have a Differentiator

Sometimes the mechanism extraction produces something genuinely generic. The expert is good at what they do but their approach isn't meaningfully different from others in their specialty. This is more common with newer practitioners or those in commoditized specialties.

**The dig-deeper protocol:**

1. **Ask about their best result:** "Tell me about the patient you're most proud of. The one where you got a result that surprised even you." — Often the differentiation is hiding in their outcomes, not their process.

2. **Ask about their frustration with the industry:** "What do most [chiropractors / functional medicine docs / dentists] get wrong?" — Their criticism of others often reveals what they do differently.

3. **Ask about their learning:** "What's the thing you learned or discovered that changed how you practice — that you wish you'd known from day one?" — This often surfaces the epiphany that IS the new vehicle.

4. **Ask about patient language:** "What do your patients say about you that they wouldn't say about the last provider they saw?" — Patient language often captures the differentiation the expert can't articulate themselves.

If all four produce generic responses, flag `mechanism.differentiation_strength: low` and escalate to the Brand Strategy agent as a top priority. The Brand Strategy agent can work with the expert to develop a named mechanism and sharpen the positioning — but the onboarding agent must be honest about the starting point rather than manufacturing false specificity.

## Part 3: The One Big Domino — The Belief That Makes Everything Else Inevitable

### What It Is

From Russell Brunson: in every market, there is a single belief that, if the prospect accepts it, makes every other objection irrelevant. This is the "One Big Domino."

**Health example:** If the patient accepts "my body isn't broken — it's stuck in a pattern that nobody identified," then:
- They stop believing their condition is permanent → objection removed
- They stop comparing this to previous failed treatments → objection removed
- They become curious about what the pattern is → interest created
- They see the expert as the person who can identify the pattern → authority established
- They're willing to try a new approach → action enabled

One belief shift. Every objection falls.

### Why It Must Be Captured in Onboarding

The One Big Domino is the atomic unit of the entire messaging system. It determines:
- What the expert leads with in every piece of content
- What belief the webinar is designed to install
- What the email sequence systematically builds toward
- What the ad hook creates curiosity about
- What the Brand North Star is anchored to

If the onboarding agent captures a vague One Big Domino ("health is connected to lifestyle"), every downstream output will be vague. If it captures a specific, belief-shifting One Big Domino ("your thyroid isn't failing — your immune system is attacking it, and nobody told you why"), every output has a spine.

### How to Extract It

**The question:** "What's the one thing that, if your ideal patient truly believed it, would change everything for them? The belief that, once they accept it, all their other questions and doubts start to fall away?"

**Follow-up if the answer is too broad:** "Make it specific to YOUR patients. Not 'health is about root cause' — every functional medicine doc says that. What's the specific root cause belief that changes the game for YOUR particular patients?"

**Follow-up if still generic:** "Think about the last patient who had a breakthrough moment in your office — the moment their face changed. What did you tell them that shifted everything?"

**What gets captured:**

| Dossier Field | Example |
|---|---|
| `brand.one_big_domino` | "Your body already knows how to heal. The problem isn't that it's broken — it's that the signals telling it to stay sick haven't been identified and interrupted." |
| `brand.one_big_domino.supporting_beliefs` | ["Standard testing misses 80% of what's actually happening", "Symptoms are messages, not malfunctions", "Every chronic condition has an upstream trigger that can be identified"] |
| `brand.one_big_domino.opposing_beliefs` | ["My condition is genetic and permanent", "I've tried everything — nothing works for me", "If it was fixable, my doctor would have found it by now"] |

The `opposing_beliefs` field is especially critical — these are the exact limiting beliefs that the Content Builder targets in awareness-level content, the Email Builder breaks in nurture sequences, and the Webinar Builder demolishes in the belief-breaking section of the Perfect Webinar framework.

## Part 4: The Epiphany Bridge Story — The Expert's Origin

### What It Is

From Expert Secrets: an Epiphany Bridge is a story that takes the listener from their current belief to a new belief — not through logic, but through the experience of someone who had the same old belief and discovered the new one.

The most powerful Epiphany Bridge is the expert's own origin story: the moment they discovered the approach they now use. Not "I went to school for functional medicine." The actual moment. The patient who changed everything. The research paper that rewrote their understanding. The personal health crisis that sent them looking for answers outside the system.

### Why It Must Be Captured in Onboarding

The expert's origin story is used in:
- The opening of every webinar (establishes credibility and relatability)
- The "About" section of every funnel page (builds trust)
- Specific content pieces designed for deep engagement (shares, saves, comments)
- The long-form email that converts the hardest skeptics (story > logic for belief change)
- Podcast episode structures (narrative arc)

An expert without a documented origin story will have all of these assets built from generic templates. An expert with a vivid, emotionally specific origin story will have assets that feel human and convert at significantly higher rates.

### How to Extract It

**The question:** "Take me back to the moment you realized your approach was different. Was it a specific patient? A research discovery? A personal experience? What happened?"

**What the agent listens for:**
- **The "Before" state:** What the expert believed or did before the epiphany (this is where the audience identifies — "I was just like every other doctor, prescribing the standard protocol")
- **The trigger event:** What happened that created the shift (a patient who shouldn't have recovered did; a test revealed something nobody expected; the expert's own health crisis)
- **The "After" realization:** What the expert now understands that they didn't before (the new vehicle discovery moment)
- **The emotional truth:** How it felt — the confusion, the excitement, the sense of responsibility
- **The bridge to patients:** Why this moment made them committed to doing things differently for every patient after

**What gets captured:**

| Dossier Field | Content |
|---|---|
| `story.origin.before_state` | "I was running a standard chiropractic practice. Adjustments, follow-ups, the usual. I was good at it — but I knew something was missing." |
| `story.origin.trigger_event` | "A patient came in who had been everywhere. Six doctors. Nothing worked. I ran a test nobody else had thought to run — and it showed exactly what was wrong." |
| `story.origin.realization` | "That's when I understood — I wasn't just fixing alignment. I was identifying patterns in the nervous system that nobody else was looking for." |
| `story.origin.emotional_truth` | "It was equal parts exciting and terrifying. I realized I was onto something, but I also realized nobody had taught me this in school." |
| `story.origin.bridge_to_patient` | "If I hadn't found this, that patient would still be suffering. And there are thousands more like her." |

### The Character Type Inference

From the origin story, the onboarding agent also infers which of Brunson's three character types the expert naturally embodies:

1. **The Reluctant Hero** — "I didn't set out to do this. I was pushed into it because I saw something that wasn't right."
2. **The One with a Cause** — "This became my mission. I couldn't NOT do this once I understood what was possible."
3. **The Reporter** — "I documented everything. I became obsessed with learning and sharing what I found."

This character type informs how the Content Builder frames authority, how the Webinar Builder opens the presentation, and how the Email Builder structures the nurture sequence narrative voice.

| Dossier Field | Value |
|---|---|
| `brand.character_type` | `reluctant_hero` \| `cause_driven` \| `reporter` |

## Part 5: The Proof Bank — Patient Transformation Stories, Social Proof Inventory, and Evidence Stack

### Why the Proof Bank Is Non-Negotiable

Every close in every format — webinar, sales page, email, ad, phone call — depends on proof. The expert's claims mean nothing without evidence that the approach actually works for real people. The deeper the proof bank, the more conversion firepower every downstream agent has.

The onboarding agent captures as much proof as possible in the initial session and flags gaps for the Proof Bank Miner sub-agent to fill from existing content.

### Patient Transformation Stories

The agent needs a minimum of 2–3 structured transformation stories. These are not generic testimonials — they are full narrative arcs.

**Extraction question:** "Tell me about a patient result you're most proud of. Someone who came in skeptical, had a real problem, and walked out with a real result."

**Follow-up probes (one at a time):**
- "What was their life like before they found you?"
- "What had they already tried?"
- "What was the moment they realized things were changing?"
- "How long did it take?"
- "What did they say — their actual words?"

**For each story, capture:**

| Dossier Field | What It Contains |
|---|---|
| `proof_bank.transformation_stories[].avatar_match` | Which persona this patient maps to |
| `proof_bank.transformation_stories[].before_state` | Vivid description of life before the expert |
| `proof_bank.transformation_stories[].treatments_tried` | What failed before — creates "I've tried everything" resonance |
| `proof_bank.transformation_stories[].turning_point` | The specific moment of change |
| `proof_bank.transformation_stories[].result` | Specific, measurable outcome |
| `proof_bank.transformation_stories[].timeline` | How long to first result and full result |
| `proof_bank.transformation_stories[].direct_quote` | Patient's actual words — the most valuable proof element |
| `proof_bank.transformation_stories[].media_available` | Video testimonial, before/after photos, lab results |

**Special story types to seek:**
- **Most dramatic transformation** — the biggest before/after gap (priority for webinar proof stack and high-impact ads)
- **Fastest result** — shortest time to noticeable change (maximizes the Hormozi "time delay" value equation score)
- **Skeptic-to-believer** — the patient who was most resistant and still got results (most powerful for Solution-Aware audiences comparing options)

### Social Proof Inventory

Beyond stories, catalog what exists:

**Extraction question:** "What proof assets do you have? Think reviews, video testimonials, before-and-after documentation, published research, media features, speaking appearances."

| Dossier Field | What It Captures |
|---|---|
| `proof_bank.testimonials_text` | Written testimonials with source context |
| `proof_bank.video_testimonials` | Video testimonial references (upload later) |
| `proof_bank.before_after_data` | Quantitative before/after data points (lab values, metrics) |
| `proof_bank.media_mentions` | Press, podcast features, publications (Web Scraper pre-fills) |
| `proof_bank.collective_results_stats` | Aggregate outcome data (patients treated, avg time to result, success rate) |
| `proof_bank.credentials_and_certifications` | Relevant credentials, board certifications, training |
| `proof_bank.proof_bank_depth_score` | Internal score 0–100 reflecting proof richness |

**If the proof bank is thin (<40 score):** Flag it. The Growth Strategy agent should include a "proof-building sprint" in the first 30-day plan — systematically collecting testimonials, documenting results, and building the evidence stack. The platform can produce content without proof, but it converts dramatically better with it.

## Part 6: The Competitive Landscape — What the Expert Is Positioned Against

### Why This Matters

The expert doesn't exist in a vacuum. Their patients are comparing them to other options — conventional medicine, other practitioners in their specialty, DIY approaches, doing nothing. The onboarding agent must understand what the expert is positioned against to give the Content Builder and Ad Creative Builder the "why most approaches fail" language.

### How to Extract It

**Extraction question:** "When your ideal patient is looking for help before they find you — what are the other options they're considering? And what do they typically try first?"

**Follow-up:** "When they come to you after trying those — what do they tell you about why it didn't work?"

**What gets captured:**

| Dossier Field | Example |
|---|---|
| `competitive_landscape.conventional_path` | "PCP prescribes levothyroxine. Endocrinologist says levels are 'normal.' Patient still feels terrible." |
| `competitive_landscape.alternative_competitors` | "Other functional medicine docs who run the same basic panel but don't go deep enough on the immune component." |
| `competitive_landscape.diy_approaches` | "Google protocols, supplement stacks from Instagram influencers, elimination diets without testing." |
| `competitive_landscape.common_failure_modes` | ["Treated the symptom not the cause", "Ran basic labs that missed the real picture", "Gave a generic protocol not personalized to their situation"] |
| `competitive_landscape.patient_language_about_failures` | ["They told me it was just stress", "My labs were normal but I still feel awful", "Nobody would listen to me"] |

The `patient_language_about_failures` field is pure gold for the Content Builder — these are the exact phrases that stop someone mid-scroll because they feel seen.

## Part 7: The Offer Stack Extraction — Not What They Sell, But What Transformation They Deliver

### The Critical Reframe

Most experts describe their offers as features: "It's a 12-week program with weekly calls, lab testing, a supplement protocol, and meal plans." This is the *worst* possible way to capture an offer because it tells the Builder agents nothing about how to sell it.

**The onboarding agent must reframe every offer in terms of transformation.**

Not: "12-week program, $2,400"
But: "From 'I've tried everything and nothing works' to 'I can't believe how different I feel' in 12 weeks, with the expert personally guiding every step."

### The Extraction Framework

For each offer tier (Entry → Core → Premium), the onboarding agent extracts:

**1. The Before State** — Where is the patient when they encounter this offer?
- What symptoms are they experiencing?
- What have they already tried?
- What do they believe about their condition?
- What emotional state are they in?

**2. The After State** — Where are they when this offer delivers its transformation?
- What specific, measurable change have they experienced?
- What can they do that they couldn't before?
- How do they feel? (Not "better" — specific)
- What story do they tell their friends?

**3. The Mechanism Bridge** — How does this offer deliver the transformation?
- What is the process? (Not the features — the process the patient experiences)
- What happens first? What's the first win they feel?
- What's the key moment of insight or progress?
- How long does it take to feel the shift?

**4. The Value Stack** — What does the patient actually receive?
- Core deliverable (the main thing)
- Each bonus (with its own transformation statement)
- Guarantee (risk reversal — what happens if it doesn't work?)
- Urgency element (why now vs. later?)

### The Grand Slam Offer Test

From Alex Hormozi's $100M Offers, every offer is evaluated against the Value Equation:

> **Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

The onboarding agent doesn't lecture the expert on this framework. But it uses it internally to evaluate the captured offer:

- **Dream Outcome:** Is the "After State" specific and emotionally compelling enough?
- **Perceived Likelihood:** Does the offer include enough proof elements (testimonials, mechanism explanation, credentials) to overcome skepticism?
- **Time Delay:** Is the time to first result clearly communicated? Is there an early quick win?
- **Effort & Sacrifice:** Is the patient's required effort reasonable and clearly defined?

If the offer scores weak on any dimension, the onboarding agent flags it: `offer.value_equation_flag: time_delay_unclear` — not to lecture the expert, but to ensure the Brand Strategy and Funnel Builder agents can strengthen those dimensions in the messaging.

## Part 8: The Avatar Depth Extraction — Beyond Demographics Into Psychographics

### The Four Layers

Demographics (age, location, income) are captured quickly and treated as context, not content. The real value is in the four psychological layers:

#### Layer 1: The Symptom Stack
What the avatar *experiences* — not diagnoses, but daily reality.

**Good extraction:** "Can't make it to 2pm without crashing. Brain fog so bad she can't finish a sentence. Has to cancel plans with her kids at least once a week. Wakes up more tired than when she went to sleep."

**Weak extraction:** "Fatigue. Brain fog. Low energy."

The difference between these two is the difference between an ad hook that stops someone mid-scroll and one they keep scrolling past. Specificity is everything.

**When the expert gives a weak (vague) symptom stack:** Don't accept it. Probe: "Walk me through a bad day for this person — from the moment they wake up. What's the first thing they notice? What falls apart by lunch? What do they cancel in the evening?"

#### Layer 2: The Emotional Core
What the avatar *feels* underneath the symptoms — the emotional experience of living in their condition.

**The four emotional layers to extract:**
1. **Frustration** — What specifically makes them angry about their situation?
2. **Fear** — What are they afraid will happen if nothing changes?
3. **Shame** — What have they stopped telling people? What do they hide?
4. **Hope** — What do they secretly still believe might be possible? (This is where the expert meets them)

#### Layer 3: The Belief System
What the avatar *believes* about their condition, their body, and their options.

**Critical beliefs to capture:**
- What do they believe is causing their problem?
- What do they believe is possible for them?
- What have they been told by other providers?
- What objections would they have to the expert's approach?

These beliefs become the specific limiting beliefs that the Content Builder targets in awareness-level content, the Email Builder breaks in nurture sequences, and the Webinar Builder demolishes in the belief-breaking section of the Perfect Webinar framework.

#### Layer 4: The Internal Dialogue
What the avatar *says to themselves* — the voice in their head that the messaging must speak to.

**Example internal dialogues:**
- *"Maybe this is just what getting older feels like."*
- *"I've tried everything. Nothing works for me."*
- *"My doctor said this is genetic. There's nothing I can do."*
- *"I just want to feel like myself again."*

When the Content Builder opens a post with one of these exact phrases, the avatar feels *seen*. That's the moment engagement happens — not from clever copy, but from accurate psychological extraction in onboarding.

**Extraction question for internal dialogue:** "What's the thing your ideal patient says to themselves at 2am — the thing they think but don't say out loud to anyone?"

### The 5 Levels of Awareness — Determining Entry Angle

Eugene Schwartz's 5 Levels of Awareness determine how every piece of content opens. The onboarding agent must determine where the expert's primary audience sits:

| Level | Description | Content Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1: Unaware** | Doesn't know they have a problem worth solving | Lead with aspirational future. Vision content. "Imagine waking up with energy..." |
| **Level 2: Problem-Aware** | Knows the problem, doesn't know solutions exist | Lead with validation and pain. "You're not crazy. Here's why you feel this way." |
| **Level 3: Solution-Aware** | Knows solutions exist, hasn't chosen one | Lead with mechanism differentiation. "Here's why most approaches fail." |
| **Level 4: Product-Aware** | Knows the expert's specific offer | Lead with proof and specifics. "Here's what happened for Sarah in 8 weeks." |
| **Level 5: Most Aware** | Ready to buy, just needs the push | Lead with urgency and logistics. "Spots open Tuesday. Here's how to get started." |

**Default content distribution:** 40% Level 2 / 35% Level 3 / 25% Level 4 — because most cold traffic in health is problem-aware but not solution-aware. The expert knows their audience best — the onboarding agent confirms this distribution or adjusts based on the expert's input.

**Extraction question:** "When someone first discovers you — on Instagram, through a Google search, from a friend's recommendation — how much do they already know? Are they just realizing something's wrong? Have they been to other providers? Or do they already know about functional medicine and they're comparing options?"

## Part 9: The Voice DNA Mass Ingestion — Pre-Session Intelligence

### The Writing vs. Speaking Split

Most experts communicate differently across contexts. Their Instagram captions may be polished and measured. Their live videos or podcast appearances may be punchy, raw, and more emotionally expressive. Their website copy (often written by someone else) may sound nothing like them at all.

The Voice DNA Mass Ingestion sub-agent runs before the onboarding session and must flag when a significant voice split exists.

**Detection method:** Compare vocabulary, sentence length, formality level, and emotional intensity across content types. If the voice profile from Instagram captions differs significantly from YouTube/podcast transcripts, flag:

`voice_dna.writing_vs_speaking_difference: true`

With a note describing the split: "Written content is more polished and clinical. Spoken content is more casual, punchy, and emotionally direct."

**How the agent uses this in the session:**

When presenting the Voice DNA confirmation, surface the split explicitly:
> "I noticed something interesting — your written content sounds more measured and clinical, but when you're on video you're much more direct and emotionally expressive. Which one feels more like the real you? That's the voice we want to build from."

Most experts will point to their spoken voice as the authentic one. The written voice has usually been edited by someone else or self-censored for "professionalism." The platform should almost always build from the spoken/natural voice and elevate it — not from the polished/edited voice.

**What gets captured:**

| Dossier Field | Content |
|---|---|
| `voice_dna.writing_vs_speaking_difference` | true/false |
| `voice_dna.primary_voice_source` | `spoken` \| `written` \| `no_difference` |
| `voice_dna.voice_split_description` | "Written: measured, third-person, clinical. Spoken: first-person, punchy, uses humor and frustration." |

### Reconciliation with Live Session Data

After the session, the Voice DNA Analyst sub-agent merges:
1. **Mass ingestion profile** — statistical, high-volume, cross-content (the base)
2. **Session capture** — spontaneous, real-time, includes corrections and reframes (the refinement)
3. **Expert confirmation** — the expert's own judgment about what sounds like them (the lock)

Conflicts between the three sources are resolved by the expert's confirmation. If they say "the punchy version is me," that's the voice — regardless of what the statistical analysis showed from their polished blog posts.

## Part 10: The Enrichment Session Protocol

### When It's Needed

An enrichment session is scheduled when:
- The initial session was Fast-Start and Tier 2/3 dossier fields remain empty
- `enrichment_priority: high` was flagged
- The proof bank depth score is below 40
- The expert was Archetype 3 (Overwhelmed) and gave abbreviated answers
- Key fields like `mechanism.name` or `brand.one_big_domino.opposing_beliefs` are missing

### How It Differs from the Initial Session

The enrichment session is NOT a redo. The expert should never feel like they're starting over or that the first session failed. The frame is: "The platform has been working with what you gave me. Now I need a few specific things to make your content even sharper."

**Opening:**
> "Since our last session, I've built your first batch of content and your strategy is taking shape. There are a few specific areas where deeper input from you would make a real difference. This should take about 15 minutes."

**Structure:**
1. Show what's been built since the first session (builds momentum and proves value)
2. Ask the 3–5 specific questions that fill the highest-value gaps
3. Capture 1–2 patient transformation stories if proof bank is thin
4. Confirm or refine any fields flagged as `[INFERRED]`
5. Close with the updated dossier score showing the improvement

**Key principle:** The enrichment session should feel like a conversation with a colleague who's already doing great work and just needs a few inputs — not like a second intake interview.

## Part 11: Onboarding Quality Checklist — What "Done" Looks Like

Before the onboarding agent marks `handoff_ready: true`, every field in this checklist must be populated or explicitly flagged as `enrichment_needed`:

### Tier 1: Must Have (blocks handoff if missing)
- [ ] `voice.tone_primary` — Confirmed by expert, not just inferred
- [ ] `voice.signature_phrases` — Minimum 3, confirmed
- [ ] `voice.words_they_avoid` — Minimum 2, confirmed
- [ ] `voice.sample_extracts` — Minimum 3 verbatim quotes from session or content
- [ ] `mechanism.core_approach` — Specific, not generic
- [ ] `mechanism.natural_language` — Expert's own words
- [ ] `brand.one_big_domino` — Specific belief statement
- [ ] `avatar.primary` — At least one fully fleshed avatar with all 4 psychological layers
- [ ] `offers.entry` — At least one offer with Before State, After State, and price
- [ ] `story.origin.trigger_event` — The expert's epiphany moment
- [ ] `competitive_landscape.common_failure_modes` — What other approaches get wrong

### Tier 2: Should Have (flags enrichment if missing)
- [ ] `voice.humor_level` — Detected and confirmed
- [ ] `voice.authority_style` — Identified (credential / results / story / teaching)
- [ ] `voice_dna.writing_vs_speaking_difference` — Detected from mass ingestion
- [ ] `mechanism.name` — Named or flagged for Brand Strategy naming
- [ ] `mechanism.vs_conventional` — Clear contrast articulated
- [ ] `brand.one_big_domino.opposing_beliefs` — At least 3 limiting beliefs identified

- [ ] `proof_bank.transformation_stories` — At least 2 structured stories

- [ ] `avatar.tertiary` — Third avatar
- [ ] `voice.content_type_calibrations` — How voice shifts across IG, email, webinar

## Part 12: Implementation Guide for the Engineering Team

### Context Injection Mapping

This kit informs the following injection points in the IQ Claw prompt variants:

| Prompt Placeholder | Kit Section That Feeds It |
|---|---|
| `{voice_dna}` | Part 9 (Mass Ingestion) + Step 4 Voice DNA methodology |
| `{brand_north_star}` | Part 3 (One Big Domino) + Part 2 (New Vehicle) + Part 4 (Character Type) |
| `{personas}` | Part 8 (Avatar Depth Extraction) + Part 6 (Competitive Landscape patient language) |
| `{offers}` | Part 7 (Offer Stack Extraction) |
| `{feedback_history}` | Part 1 (Expert Archetypes inform initial calibration) |
| `{practitioner_state}` | Part 11 (Quality Checklist → onboarding progress_pct and section status) |

### Onboarding Plan Step Enhancement

The planner module's `onboarding_fast_start` (7 steps) and `onboarding_deep_dive` (15 steps) plans should incorporate:

1. **Expert archetype detection** (Part 1) — Run classification in first 2–3 exchanges. Store as `session.expert_archetype`. Use to calibrate question style, response length expectations, and pacing for the remainder of the session.

2. **New Vehicle extraction** (Part 2) — Mandatory step. Cannot be deferred. If the extraction produces generic results, run the dig-deeper protocol (4 follow-up questions). If still generic, flag `mechanism.differentiation_strength: low` and proceed.

3. **One Big Domino extraction** (Part 3) — Mandatory step. This is the keystone. If the initial answer is broad, deploy the two follow-up probes before accepting the field value.

4. **Proof Bank extraction** (Part 5) — Capture at least 2 transformation stories and inventory existing proof assets. If proof bank depth score < 40, flag for Growth Strategy to include proof-building sprint in first 30-day plan.

5. **Competitive Landscape extraction** (Part 6) — New mandatory step not in current plan. Surfaces the "why other approaches fail" language that Level 3 content depends on.

6. **Origin story extraction** (Part 4) — If time-constrained in Fast-Start, this is the first candidate for enrichment session deferral, but it should be attempted.

7. **Offer transformation reframe** (Part 7) — Never accept feature-list descriptions as the final offer capture. Always push for Before State → After State framing.

8. **4-layer avatar extraction** (Part 8) — Demographics are fast-start; psychological layers are where the agent should invest time. If the expert gives vague symptom descriptions, use the "walk me through a bad day" probe.

9. **Quality checklist validation** (Part 11) — Run before `handoff_ready: true`. Tier 1 fields block handoff. Tier 2 fields flag enrichment. Tier 3 fields recommend enrichment session.

### Quality Gate Addition

Add a **Dossier Depth QA** gate to the existing Voice QA and Brand QA pipeline. This gate evaluates the completeness and specificity of the dossier before Layer 2 begins strategy generation.

| Check | Pass Criteria | Fail Action |
|---|---|---|
| Voice DNA specificity | Sample extracts are *verbatim quotes*, not paraphrased summaries | Return to onboarding agent with instruction to capture raw quotes |
| Mechanism clarity | A non-expert could explain the expert's approach after reading the mechanism fields | Flag for dig-deeper protocol or enrichment session |
| Avatar depth | Emotional core and internal dialogue layers are populated with specific language, not just symptom labels | Return with instruction to probe for specificity |
| Offer transformation | Every offer has a Before State and After State, not just features and price | Return with instruction to reframe through transformation lens |
| One Big Domino precision | The belief statement is specific to THIS expert, not a generic category statement (fails if it could apply to any practitioner in the same specialty) | Return with follow-up probes |
| Proof bank minimum | At least 2 transformation stories captured OR proof_bank_depth_score >= 30 | Flag enrichment priority, do not block handoff |
| Competitive landscape | At least 3 `common_failure_modes` captured | Flag enrichment priority, do not block handoff |

**Pass threshold:** All non-proof, non-competitive checks must pass. Proof and competitive checks are flagged but do not block handoff — because these can be built from mass ingestion and existing content.

### Enrichment Session Triggering

When `handoff_ready: true` is set but enrichment flags exist:
1. Queue an enrichment session notification for 5–7 days after initial onboarding
2. Include in the notification: what content has been produced since onboarding (demonstrates value) and what specific inputs would make it better (creates clear reason to return)
3. The enrichment session uses the protocol in Part 10
4. After enrichment, re-run Dossier Depth QA and update the quality scores
