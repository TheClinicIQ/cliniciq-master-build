# ClinicIQ — Hooks for All 25 Video Scripts
## Facebook & Instagram · Text Overlay + Verbal Hook for Each Ad

**Rules applied to every hook:**
- Text overlay: 6 words max, stops the scroll, works with zero sound
- Verbal hook: first 3 seconds, spoken — pulls them off the pause and into the video
- Burning question: specific, answerable, and aligned to where the VSL closes the loop
- Identity filter: speaks to ambitious health experts, not consumers, not beginners

---

## UNAWARE (UA-01 through UA-05)
*Meet them at their life. No AI, no jargon. Just the collision course they don't know they're on.*

---

### UA-01
**Script angle:** Best clinicians hitting a wall. Business model built for a world that no longer exists. Market shifting faster than anyone prepared for.

**Text overlay:**
> "The business model you're running is outdated."

**Verbal hook:**
> "Some of the best clinicians I know are hitting a wall right now — and it has nothing to do with how good they are."

**Burning question planted:** *What changed — and why didn't anyone warn me?*

**Why this works:** Leads with identity validation ("best clinicians") then creates a threat they can't see. The word "outdated" on screen stops practitioners mid-scroll because it feels personally directed. No AI language. No pitch. Pure pattern interrupt.

---

### UA-02
**Script angle:** You got into health to help people. It became ads, leads, content, managing a team, doing your own books.

**Text overlay:**
> "You didn't sign up for any of this."

**Verbal hook:**
> "You got into health to help people. Somewhere along the way it turned into something completely different."

**Burning question planted:** *Is there a way to get back to just doing the medicine — and have everything else handled?*

**Why this works:** "You didn't sign up for any of this" is 7 words of pure recognition. Every health expert who is doing non-clinical work reads this and feels seen before a single word is spoken. Identity-first, problem-second. The verbal hook extends the loop without closing it.

---

### UA-03
**Script angle:** Two types of practices in 2026. Most don't know which type they are.

**Text overlay:**
> "Two types of health practices in 2026."

**Verbal hook:**
> "In 2026 there are two types of health practices. Most people don't know which one they are yet."

**Burning question planted:** *Which type am I — and what's the difference?*

**Why this works:** Creates an immediate categorization anxiety. "Which one am I?" is an almost involuntary question. The number "2026" makes it feel current and urgent. No explanation given — the withholding is the mechanism that drives clicks.

---

### UA-04
**Script angle:** Private health practice has about a year before the AI gap becomes impossible to close. Watching it happen in real time.

**Text overlay:**
> "The gap is forming right now."

**Verbal hook:**
> "I'm going to say something that might sound dramatic — but I need you to hear it."

**Burning question planted:** *What gap? How big is it — and am I already on the wrong side?*

**Why this works:** "The gap is forming right now" is urgent without being specific — it demands elaboration. The verbal hook uses the exact script language ("might sound dramatic") which signals that what's coming is real, not hype. The combination of visual urgency + verbal credibility-building stops the right person and filters out casual scrollers.

---

### UA-05
**Script angle:** No warning when your market shifts. One day you just notice things getting harder — new patients harder, ads less effective, unknown practices showing up everywhere.

**Text overlay:**
> "Nobody warns you when your market shifts."

**Verbal hook:**
> "Nobody's going to warn you when your market shifts. There's no announcement. No memo. One day you just notice."

**Burning question planted:** *Is my market shifting right now without me knowing it?*

**Why this works:** This is the most cinematic hook in the unaware set. "Nobody warns you" taps straight into the fear of being blindsided — which is more visceral than the fear of a known threat. The repetition between overlay and verbal is intentional: it doubles the impact for people watching with sound. Short. Ominous. Specific enough to feel personal.

## PROBLEM AWARE (PA-01 through PA-05)
*They know something's broken. Name the pain so specifically they feel diagnosed, not sold.*

---

### PA-01
**Script angle:** Offer not built around what patients want to buy. Consultation priced wrong. No KPIs. One-on-one dependent. AI strategy is ChatGPT when they remember.

**Text overlay:**
> "Every fix you've tried hasn't stuck. Here's why."

**Verbal hook:**
> "Let me tell you exactly what's broken in your health business — and why the fixes you've tried haven't worked."

**Burning question planted:** *What IS the actual root cause — and why has nothing fixed it?*

**Why this works:** "Every fix you've tried hasn't stuck" is a mirror. Any health expert who has hired an agency, bought a course, or tried a new funnel and had it fail will stop scrolling. The verbal hook promises a diagnosis, not a pitch. The phrase "hasn't worked" does more work than "isn't working" — past tense implies a pattern, not a moment.

---

### PA-02
**Script angle:** Five numbers run every practice. CPL, opt-in rate, purchase rate, CAC, LTV. Most don't know any of them so every decision is a guess.

**Text overlay:**
> "5 numbers run your practice. Know any of them?"

**Verbal hook:**
> "Five numbers determine whether your practice grows or bleeds — and most clinicians don't know a single one of them."

**Burning question planted:** *What are the 5 numbers — and what are mine telling me right now?*

**Why this works:** The overlay is a direct challenge that's impossible to scroll past without self-checking. The question format ("Know any of them?") creates mild shame that converts to curiosity. "Grows or bleeds" is deliberately binary — it removes the comfortable middle ground. Any practitioner running paid media stops here because they know they should know these numbers.

---

### PA-03
**Script angle:** 400+ consultations per day. Not because better clinicians. Stopped pricing consultations with ego, stopped treating them as revenue events.

**Text overlay:**
> "400 consultations a day. Not because we're better doctors."

**Verbal hook:**
> "We sell over 400 consultations a day. Not because we're better clinicians. Here's the actual reason."

**Burning question planted:** *What IS the reason — and can I replicate it?*

**Why this works:** The number "400" is the scroll-stopper. It's specific, verifiable, and creates an immediate gap between "my calendar" and "their calendar." The disqualifier — "not because we're better doctors" — removes the defensive reaction ("well they're more famous") and replaces it with pure curiosity. The promise "here's the actual reason" makes the click feel like getting an answer, not watching an ad.

---

### PA-04
**Script angle:** High-ticket program not selling — not the price. It's offer framing, value equation, bonuses as random add-ons, consultation structure that meanders.

**Text overlay:**
> "Your high-ticket offer isn't selling. It's not the price."

**Verbal hook:**
> "The reason your high-ticket program isn't converting has nothing to do with what you're charging."

**Burning question planted:** *Then what IS the reason — and can I fix it without changing the price?*

**Why this works:** "It's not the price" is a belief pattern interrupt. Almost every practitioner with an unselling high-ticket offer has told themselves it's too expensive. Dismantling that assumption in the overlay creates instant relief-plus-curiosity: "If it's not the price, what is it?" The relief keeps them from leaving defensively; the curiosity drives the click.

---

### PA-05
**Script angle:** If you disappeared for 30 days, what happens to revenue? Practices compounding built operating systems. Infrastructure problem, not marketing problem.

**Text overlay:**
> "If you vanished for 30 days — what happens to your revenue?"

**Verbal hook:**
> "If you disappeared completely — no posts, no emails, no ads — what would happen to your revenue? Answer that honestly."

**Burning question planted:** *Is my practice actually scalable — or am I the bottleneck?*

**Why this works:** This is a thought experiment that feels like a personal audit. "Answer that honestly" is a slight challenge that makes the viewer feel like the ad is reading their mind. Anyone who runs a practice that depends on their daily presence already knows the answer is uncomfortable — the hook just makes them admit it to themselves. Pure infrastructure pain made visceral.

## SOLUTION AWARE (SA-01 through SA-05)
*They've tried AI. They think they're current. Show them they're playing checkers while this is chess.*

---

### SA-01
**Script angle:** Already using AI, but it's not a strategy — it's a faster way to do manual tasks. Still requires you. When you stop prompting, it stops.

**Text overlay:**
> "Using AI tools is not an AI strategy."

**Verbal hook:**
> "I have a lot of respect for the fact that you're already using AI. And I need to tell you something important about what you're actually doing."

**Burning question planted:** *If what I'm doing isn't an AI strategy — what is one?*

**Why this works:** The overlay immediately challenges an identity the viewer holds ("I'm already on top of this") without being aggressive. The verbal hook opens with validation ("I have a lot of respect for...") before the disruption — which keeps the SA-level viewer from dismissing the ad as basic content they've already seen. The "something important" withhold is the mechanism that pulls them into the video.

---

### SA-02
**Script angle:** Open-CLAW does to health businesses what iPhone did to Nokia. Not a better version — a different category. Observes, identifies, proposes, implements, measures, learns. Automatically.

**Text overlay:**
> "This is the iPhone moment for health businesses."

**Verbal hook:**
> "Open-CLAW is going to do to health businesses what the iPhone did to Nokia. It's not a better version of what you're using. It's a completely different category."

**Burning question planted:** *What makes it categorically different — and why does that gap matter for my practice?*

**Why this works:** "iPhone moment" on the overlay is culturally loaded — every person alive understands what that means for the companies that missed it. For the solution-aware viewer who thinks they're current on AI, this hook creates a productive paranoia: "Am I Nokia?" The verbal hook delivers the category disruptor (not better, different) immediately, before they can skip.

---

### SA-03
**Script angle:** You're using AI, saving time, producing more — but is your business actually smarter or more profitable than it was 6 months ago?

**Text overlay:**
> "You're using AI. Is your business smarter?"

**Verbal hook:**
> "Here's the question nobody in the AI space is asking about your health practice: you're saving time, producing more — but is your business actually smarter than it was six months ago?"

**Burning question planted:** *What would it look like if my business was actually LEARNING from itself — and why isn't mine doing that?*

**Why this works:** The overlay is a gut-check disguised as a question. The word "smarter" reframes what AI is supposed to do — not just faster, but learning and improving. For the SA practitioner who has been equating AI with productivity, this creates a new metric they haven't been measuring. The "nobody's asking" framing positions the viewer as someone who's about to get information others aren't getting.

**Burning question planted:** *What are the five layers — and which layer am I actually at?*

For SA-level viewers who consider themselves advanced, being told they're "stuck at layer 1" is the specific challenge that makes the click feel like self-defense. The overlay works with or without sound, which is critical for FB/IG autoplay.

## PRODUCT AWARE (PR-01 through PR-05)
*They know Dr. Axe. Lead with full conviction. No education needed. Make them feel the gravity of the opportunity.*

---

### PR-01
**Script angle:** Made a decision. $2M, all in on AI infrastructure. Google engineers, government AI teams. First agentic OS for health experts. Health 3.0.

**Text overlay:**
> "I put $2M into something I've never talked about publicly."

**Verbal hook:**
> "Over the last several months I made a decision. I took two million dollars and went all in — not on a supplement, not on a clinic, not on a media play. On something completely different. And I want to tell you what it is."

**Burning question planted:** *What did he build — and why is he telling me now?*

**Why this works:** For a product-aware audience that knows Dr. Axe, the "$2M all in" creates immediate gravity. They're already watching for what comes next. "Not on a supplement, not on a clinic" uses elimination to create tension — what ISN'T it eliminates the expected answers and forces genuine curiosity. The "I want to tell you what it is" close pulls them directly into the body of the video.

---

### PR-02

**Text overlay:**
> "We built something this industry has never had."

> "We are the first Agentic-as-a-Service company built specifically for health experts. I know that's a big claim. I'm making it because it's true."

**Burning question planted:** *What exactly is it — and what would it mean for my practice specifically?*

**Why this works:** "Something this industry has never had" is a category claim that works on both the known and unknown. The verbal hook leads with the claim, immediately acknowledges the skepticism ("I know that's a big claim"), and then doubles down — which is more credible than softening it. Product-aware viewers who've followed Dr. Axe know he doesn't oversell. The confidence signal here is the hook.

## MOST AWARE (MA-01 through MA-05)
*They know everything. They just haven't moved. Make waiting feel costly. Make the next step feel obvious.*

---

### MA-01
**Script angle:** You've seen the content. You understand the gap. You know agentic AI is real. One question remains: what's your plan when your competitor has it and you don't?

**Text overlay:**
> "You already know this is real. There's one question left."

**Verbal hook:**
> "You've seen the content. You understand the gap. You know this is coming. So let me ask you the only question that actually matters right now."

**Burning question planted:** *What IS that one question — and do I have an answer to it?*

**Why this works:** This hook is a mirror held up to inaction. "You already know this is real" is simultaneously validation (you're smart, you got it) and a subtle confrontation (so why haven't you moved?). The withhold — "there's one question left" — forces the click because nobody can ignore an unasked question that's pointed directly at them. For MA viewers who have been processing and not deciding, this hook makes waiting feel like a choice that has consequences.

---

### MA-02
**Script angle:** Window to be first isn't permanent. Direct: spent $2M, best engineers, Open-CLAW, five-layer stack. Practices getting in now compound. Ones that wait fight uphill.

**Text overlay:**
> "The window to be first in your market is closing."

**Verbal hook:**
> "The window to be first in your market with this is real — and it is not permanent. I want to be direct with you."

**Burning question planted:** *How long do I actually have — and what am I specifically risking by waiting?*

**Why this works:** "Closing" is more motivating than "closed" — there's still time, but the urgency is real. "I want to be direct with you" is a conversational signal that what's coming is unfiltered — which MA viewers, who have seen all the marketing, respond to as a pattern break. The hook creates a math problem in the viewer's mind: time remaining vs. cost of waiting. That math lives in the VSL.

---

### MA-03
**Script angle:** Two years from now you'll look back at 2026 and know exactly when the decision was made. Either you were the practice that moved, or you watched it happen.

**Text overlay:**
> "Two years from now — you'll know exactly when you decided."

**Verbal hook:**
> "Two years from now you're going to look back at 2026 and know exactly when the decision was made. The question is which side of that decision you're on."

**Burning question planted:** *What will I have wished I did — and is it too late to still be on the right side?*

**Why this works:** This hook works by projecting the viewer into a future where the decision is already made — then pulling them back to the present where they can still make it. The "which side" framing is binary and forces a choice rather than allowing comfortable ambiguity. For MA viewers who keep deferring, the future-projection technique creates a vivid, personal cost for inaction that abstract urgency language can't match. The text overlay alone is powerful enough to stop a scroll without any audio.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**For every hook:**
- Text overlay appears within the first 0.5 seconds — no cold open
- Verbal hook begins immediately; no music bed, no b-roll intro
- The speaker (Dr. Axe or on-camera talent) is on screen by second 1
- No lower-third title cards during the hook — the overlay IS the hook
- Hook text and verbal hook may mirror each other (UA-03, UA-05) or diverge to add information (PA-02, SA-01) — both strategies are intentional

**Recommended priority for first production round:**

| Priority | Script | Why |
|----------|--------|-----|
| 1 | UA-03 | Widest cold audience appeal. Lowest barrier to stop. |
| 2 | PA-01 | Highest resonance with anyone who's tried growth tactics. |
| 3 | PA-03 | 400/day stat is the most disruptive specific number in the library. |
| 4 | SA-03 | Best hook for warm audiences who use AI but aren't converting. |
| 5 | MA-03 | Strongest urgency hook for retargeting non-converters. |
| 6 | PR-03 | Leverages full Dr. Axe brand equity for warm audiences. |
| 7 | PA-05 | 30-day disappear question — highest personal resonance. |
