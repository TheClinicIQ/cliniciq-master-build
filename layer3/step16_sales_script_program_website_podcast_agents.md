## Part 1 — Sales Script Builder

### What It Is

The Sales Script Builder produces every script used in human-to-human conversion moments — phone calls, video consultations, front desk interactions, application review calls, and discovery sessions. These are the moments where the expert or their team closes high-ticket programs. A weak script here is the most expensive failure point in the entire growth engine.

This is not a general-purpose copywriting task. Sales scripts for health experts have a specific psychological architecture: the prospect arrives with damage from prior failures, active skepticism about what "another program" can do, and a deep internal desire they may not have fully articulated. The script must navigate all three before the offer is ever mentioned.

### Frameworks

The Sales Script Builder is built on the intersection of three proven frameworks, calibrated to the health expert's specific dossier:

**1. Russell Brunson's Application / Phone Sales Framework**
The four core questions that guide a high-ticket consultation:
1. What do you want? (surface goal)
2. What's preventing you from getting it? (external obstacle)
3. How long have you been dealing with this? (stakes + timeline)
4. If we could solve X, what would that mean for you? (deep desire reveal)
The script builds to the offer only after all four are answered. The sale is made at question 4 — not at the pitch.

**2. Alex Hormozi's Offer Framing**
Every script positions the program as the specific solution to the specific failure the prospect described. Not generic benefits — exact alignment between their stated problem and the program's mechanism. The close is logical, not pushy: "Based on what you told me, this is exactly what addresses that."

**3. Health-Specific Belief Architecture (from Expert Secrets synthesis)**
Health prospects arrive with a specific false belief set: "Nothing has worked before," "My condition is just how I am," "I can't afford to invest in my health." The script is structured to surface and dissolve each belief using the expert's own mechanism and proof — never arguing, always redirecting to their stated desire.

### What Gets Built

**1. Primary Phone/Video Consultation Script**
The main script for converting application leads into program enrollments. Fully written, word-for-word, with performance cues, branching paths, and objection handling built in.

Structure:
- Opening rapport (2 min) — acknowledge the application, affirm their initiative
- Discovery phase (8–12 min) — 4 Brunson questions, active listening cues, drilling to deep desire
- Diagnosis summary (2 min) — reflect back what you heard, validate their situation
- Mechanism reveal (3–5 min) — explain why what they've tried hasn't worked + the root cause approach
- Program presentation (3–5 min) — Hormozi-framed: exact alignment between their stated problem and the program's offer
- Investment conversation (2–3 min) — price, terms, payment options
- Objection handling (branching) — see Objection Library below
- Close (1–2 min) — decision moment, next step, enrollment

Total script length: 25–40 pages (word-for-word with all branches)

**2. Front Desk / Intake Script**
For practices with staff handling initial inbound calls. Shorter, tighter, goal is to qualify and book — not close.
- Qualify: budget, timeline, seriousness
- Build intrigue: acknowledge their situation, introduce the expert's approach (without full mechanism)
- Book: create urgency for the consultation appointment
Length: 8–12 pages

**3. Discovery Session Script (Assessment-Led)**
For experts who run a paid or free 1:1 assessment as the funnel entry. Different from a straight sales call — the first portion is genuinely diagnostic. The conversion comes after real findings are shared.
- Assessment questions (health-specific, from avatar psychology in dossier)
- Findings presentation — what the assessment reveals
- Bridge to program — how the program addresses the findings
- Enrollment conversation
Length: 15–20 pages

**4. Objection Library**
A full written response for every objection the expert's avatar is likely to raise, organized by objection type:
- Price objections ("I can't afford it," "I need to think about it," "I need to talk to my spouse")
- Skepticism objections ("I've tried everything," "How is this different," "I'm not sure it'll work for me")
- Timing objections ("This isn't the right time," "I'm too busy right now")
- Authority objections ("I need to check with my doctor," "What are your credentials")
- Results objections ("Can you guarantee results," "How long will it take")

Each objection gets: acknowledge → validate → reframe → bridge to desire → offer again.
The objection responses are drawn directly from the expert's own proof bank, mechanism language, and Voice DNA — every response sounds like the expert, not a generic sales script.

**5. Referral Ask Script**
The structured moment to ask for referrals — timed for peak satisfaction (typically 30–45 days into the program, after the first significant result milestone). Natural, value-first, specific.

**6. Re-enrollment / Continuity Upsell Script**
For experts with continuity programs (ongoing membership, maintenance programs). Positioned at graduation from the primary program. Not a pitch — a natural next step conversation.

### What It Is

The Program Builder is clinicIQ's end-to-end program delivery system. It builds phase-based health and wellness programs (1–6 months), delivers them through a native course player inside the IQ Platform, powers a patient AI assistant, and generates all coaching and community support content. It is the product the entire growth engine exists to sell.

This is not a course outline generator. It produces a complete, production-ready program:

- Full lesson scripts (word-for-word, in the expert's voice, ready to record)
- Workbooks and worksheets for each module
- Implementation guides (what the participant does between lessons)
- Quick reference materials (cheat sheets, protocol summaries, reference cards)
- Welcome and orientation materials
- Community and support structure spec

### The Transformation Architecture Principle

Every module and lesson is engineered around a single question: **what does the participant need to believe, know, and do at this point in their transformation journey?**

The program is not organized around what the expert knows. It is organized around what the participant experiences — the sequence of realizations, shifts, and actions that produce the result the program promises.

This distinction is critical. Expert-organized programs deliver information. Transformation-organized programs deliver results. Results create testimonials. Testimonials sell the next cohort.

The transformation arc follows a consistent structure regardless of specialty:

```
MODULE 1 — Foundation & Belief Shift
   Purpose: Establish the new vehicle. Break the false belief that "nothing works."
   Mechanism: Explain the root cause framework in plain language. Why everything 
   they've tried has failed. Why this approach is categorically different.
   Outcome: Participant believes the mechanism. Hope is activated.

MODULE 2 — Assessment & Personalization
   Purpose: Make the program specific to this participant.
   Mechanism: Assessment tools, symptom inventories, or data collection that 
   lets the expert's framework reveal the participant's specific situation.
   Outcome: Participant feels seen. The program is now theirs, not generic.

MODULES 3–N — The Protocol
   Purpose: Deliver the transformation in structured phases.
   Structure: Each module = one phase of the protocol. Each lesson = one tool/concept.
   Each lesson ends with: what you know now → what you do next → what to expect.
   Outcome: Progressive wins that build momentum and testimonial moments.

FINAL MODULE — Sustainability & Next Steps
   Purpose: Lock in the transformation. Create the identity shift.
   Mechanism: Review of progress, maintenance protocol, identity reinforcement.
   Outcome: Graduate who believes they are now the person who has solved this problem.
   Bridge: Natural setup for continuity offer, referral ask, and alumni community.
```

### Module and Lesson Architecture

**Standard module structure:**
- Module intro video script (3–5 min) — context, purpose, what's coming
- Lesson scripts (3–8 lessons per module, 10–20 min each)
- Module workbook (reflection questions, implementation exercises, tracking tools)
- Implementation guide (the specific actions between this module and the next)
- Quick reference card (one-page summary of the key framework/protocol for this module)
- Module outro script (1–2 min) — celebrate progress, preview next module, re-hook

**Lesson script structure:**
1. Re-hook (30–60 sec) — remind them why this matters, connect to their stated desire
2. Concept delivery (5–10 min) — teach one thing, explain it clearly, use the mechanism language
3. Demonstration / case study (3–5 min) — a real example or patient story that proves the concept
4. Application bridge (2–3 min) — how this specifically applies to the participant's situation
5. Action assignment (1–2 min) — one concrete action they take before the next lesson
6. Progress acknowledgment (30 sec) — affirm that completing this lesson is real progress

Every lesson script is written in the expert's exact Voice DNA — same vocabulary, same warmth level, same teaching style extracted during onboarding. A participant who has heard the expert's webinar should feel like they're continuing a conversation with the same person.

## Part 3 — Website Builder

### What It Is

The Website Builder produces the expert's core website — not the funnel pages (those are Funnel Builder territory), but the brand home: the site a prospect lands on from social, a podcast appearance, a Google search, or a referral. The website is the trust layer. It answers the question "who is this person and why should I believe them?" before the prospect enters any funnel.

### What a Health Expert's Website Actually Does

The website has one job in the conversion architecture: **warm the prospect enough to take the next step.** That step is almost always entering a funnel — opting in for the lead magnet, registering for the webinar, booking a consultation. The website is not the conversion point. The funnel is. The website is the bridge.

This means the website is not built to be comprehensive. It is built to be convincing. The expert who tries to put everything on their website builds a confusing maze. The website clinicIQ builds has one primary CTA, strong authority positioning, and a clear path forward.

### Site Architecture

```
HOME PAGE
├── Above the fold: Expert name + One-line positioning + Primary CTA
├── The Problem (Level 1–2 awareness hook — speaks to symptom, not solution)
├── Why Everything Else Fails (mechanism preview — the root cause angle)
├── The Expert's Story (abbreviated — credibility + empathy + transformation)
├── Social Proof Strip (logos, numbers, testimonial highlights)
├── The Solution Preview (introduce the program/approach — not full detail)
├── Primary CTA (webinar registration or lead magnet opt-in)
└── Secondary nav: About, Work With Me, Resources, Blog/Podcast (if applicable)

ABOUT PAGE
├── Full expert story (origin → discovery → mission)
├── Credentials and training (in plain language, not credential soup)
├── Media mentions / features / social proof
├── Personal humanizing elements (family, interests — makes them real)
└── CTA: "Here's how I work with people →"

WORK WITH ME PAGE
├── Programs overview (all tiers — which is right for whom)
├── Who this is for / who this is NOT for (specificity builds trust)
├── Application or enrollment CTA per program
└── FAQ section (top 5 objections, answered directly)

RESOURCES PAGE (optional — based on dossier content audit)
├── Lead magnet featured prominently
├── Podcast episodes (if applicable)
├── Blog posts / articles (if applicable)
├── Free training (webinar registration)

BLOG / ARTICLE PAGES (template)
├── SEO-optimized structure
├── Mechanism-consistent messaging
├── CTA embedded mid-article and at end
└── Author bio with primary CTA

CONTACT / APPLY PAGE
├── Simple form for inquiries
├── Application form for high-ticket programs (if applicable)
└── Clear response time expectation set
```

### Copy Principles

**Authority without arrogance.** The website positions the expert as the guide, not the hero. The prospect is the hero. The expert is the person who has the map.

**Mechanism-consistent.** Every page speaks the root cause language from the dossier. If the expert's mechanism is "the hormone-gut connection," that phrase — or its avatar-facing plain-language equivalent — appears on every page. Consistency of mechanism builds the category in the prospect's mind.

**Awareness-calibrated.** The home page is written for Level 1–2 awareness (symptom-aware, not solution-aware). The Work With Me page is written for Level 3–4 (solution-aware, considering options). The Apply/Enroll page is Level 5 (most aware, ready to decide).

**Voice-locked.** Same Voice DNA as every other piece of content the expert produces. A prospect who has been following the expert's content should feel at home immediately.

### Episode Architecture

The Podcast Agent builds three episode types, each with a different purpose in the awareness funnel:

**Type 1 — Solo Authority Episodes**
Expert speaks alone. Deep dives on the mechanism, specific protocols, case studies, myth-busting. Best for Level 2–3 audiences. The expert demonstrates mastery. Every solo episode ends with a CTA to the lead magnet or webinar.

Structure:
- Hook (0–90 sec): the specific problem this episode solves
- Promise (30 sec): what the listener will know by the end
- Content (15–40 min): the teaching — mechanism-consistent, actionable
- Case study (5–10 min): a real patient story illustrating the mechanism in action
- Summary (2–3 min): the 3 key takeaways
- CTA (60–90 sec): the one next step (lead magnet, webinar, apply link)

**Type 2 — Guest Interview Episodes**
Expert interviews another practitioner, researcher, or patient story. Best for Level 1–2 audiences — the guest's audience discovers the expert through the episode. Referral and partnership growth engine.

Structure:
- Host intro: guest credentials + the specific problem this conversation addresses
- Guest story: how they got into this work (builds authority and rapport)
- Core interview: 5–7 prepared questions, goes where it goes
- Key mechanism moment: host steers toward their root cause framework at least once
- Listener takeaway: "the one thing I want you to take from this..."
- CTA: host closes with their signature CTA (same as solo)

**Type 3 — Patient/Client Story Episodes**
Real transformation story. Told in the patient's voice (or reconstructed with permission). This is the most persuasive format for prospects who have tried everything and believe nothing will work. Level 3–4 audiences.

Structure:
- Context: who this person was before (avatar mirroring — listener sees themselves)
- The struggle: what they tried, why it didn't work, how bad it got
- The discovery: how they found the expert's approach
- The mechanism moment: the specific turning point when they understood the root cause
- The transformation: specific, measurable results (numbers where possible)
- The identity shift: who they are now vs. who they were
- Host bridge: how to get access to the same approach (CTA)

### What Gets Built Per Episode

1. **Episode Brief** — concept, episode type, guest (if applicable), target awareness level, primary CTA, content angle
2. **Full Script** — word-for-word for solo episodes; question set + host talking points for interviews
3. **Show Notes** — SEO-optimized, 800–1200 words, includes: episode summary, key takeaways, resources mentioned, transcript excerpt, CTA, guest bio (if applicable)
4. **Clip Spec** — the 3–5 specific moments in the script flagged for short-form repurposing, with suggested text overlays
5. **Email Announcement** — episode announcement email to the list (handed to Email Builder template)
6. **Social Post Set** — 5–10 posts pulled from episode content (routed to Content Builder)

### Season and Episode Planning

The Podcast Agent doesn't just produce individual episodes — it manages the full editorial calendar.

**Season architecture:**
Each "season" (12–16 episodes) is built around a content theme that aligns with the Monthly Master Plan campaign calendar. A season launching alongside a webinar push might be entirely mechanism-focused solo episodes and patient stories. A season in a partnership growth phase might be heavy on guest interviews.

**Episode mix default:**
- 50% solo authority episodes
- 30% guest interviews
- 20% patient/client transformation stories

Adjustable based on growth phase: partnership-heavy phase shifts to 50% guest interviews; launch phase shifts to 60% solo + patient stories.

   Writes: Clip spec for each moment — timestamp, suggested title, text overlay copy,
   platform recommendation (Reels vs. Shorts vs. TikTok)
   Routes: Clip specs to Content Builder for visual production

5. Repurposing Coordinator
   Reads: Episode script + show notes + clip specs
   Produces: Email announcement brief → handed to Email Builder
   Produces: Social post brief (10 post angles pulled from episode content) → handed to Content Builder
   Produces: YouTube description + chapter markers + thumbnail brief
   Manages: Cross-channel consistency — same episode presented correctly on every platform

6. Guest Research Agent
   Activates: When editorial calendar includes an interview episode
   Reads: Guest name/brand + Growth Blueprint target audience + niche research
   Researches: Guest's content, positioning, audience profile, recent work
   Produces: Guest research brief — who they are, their audience, their talking points,
   3–5 pre-read articles/episodes, suggested question angles
   Produces: Guest outreach template — personalized, value-first, clear ask
   Output: Guest brief → to Lead Agent for interview prep + Episode Script Writer for question development
