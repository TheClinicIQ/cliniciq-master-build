## Purpose

The Email & SMS Builder Agent produces every word that lands in the expert's audience's inbox and on their phone. This is the system's relationship channel — where cold leads become warm believers, warm believers become buyers, and buyers become ascension candidates. It is also the system's most persistent revenue channel: a well-built email list compounds over time, and a well-built sequence architecture converts that list automatically, 24 hours a day, without the expert doing anything after setup.

This agent builds, sequences, and organizes every email and SMS the expert will ever send — from the first message a new subscriber receives to the two-years-later re-engagement ping to a subscriber who went cold. Nothing is improvised. Every message is purposeful, sequenced, and calibrated to the subscriber's exact behavioral position in the funnel.

The Email Builder does not replace the expert's voice with a corporate tone or a chatbot template. Every email sounds like the expert wrote it personally — because the Voice DNA governs every word. The subscriber should never feel they're receiving automation. They should feel they're hearing from someone who understands them.

### Sequence 1: Welcome / Lead Magnet Delivery Sequence

**Trigger:** Opt-in form submission (lead magnet, free training, quiz, etc.)
**Length:** 4–5 emails
**Goal:** Deliver the lead magnet, establish the expert's authority, prime the subscriber for the webinar, and begin the belief-building arc before the first conversion event.

This sequence is the most underbuilt element in most health expert businesses. Most send one email with a download link and go quiet for a week. clinicIQ treats the post-opt-in window as the highest-engagement moment in the entire relationship — the subscriber just raised their hand. The welcome sequence capitalizes on that attention while it's still hot.

| Email | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Immediately | Lead magnet delivery + brief personal welcome + what's coming over the next few days |
| Email 2 | Day 2 | "The one thing most people miss" — the key insight from the lead magnet that most subscribers overlook; builds authority and opens a loop |
| Email 3 | Day 3 | A patient story that pre-handles the #1 objection to the webinar (addresses "I've tried things before and nothing worked") |
| Email 4 | Day 4 | Webinar invite — what they'll discover, framed as a natural next step from what they just received |
| Email 5 | Day 5 (optional) | Final webinar invite — social proof version ("2,400 people have already watched this") + soft urgency |

### Sequence 2: Pre-Webinar Engagement Sequence

**Trigger:** Webinar registration (evergreen)
**Length:** 3–4 emails
**Goal:** Keep the registered subscriber engaged until they watch. In an evergreen model, this sequence runs from registration until the subscriber either watches or falls into the no-show sequence.

The pre-webinar sequence solves a specific problem: registered subscribers who never watch. Registration intent is real but it's fragile — life interrupts. This sequence maintains the tension between registration and viewing.

| Email | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmation | Immediately | What they're about to discover (3 specific curiosity-gap bullets) + watch link + add-to-calendar prompt |
| Email 2 | Day 2 | One specific insight from the training — teased but not fully delivered. "There's one thing in the training that most [specialists] never tell their patients. I cover it at minute 23." |
| Email 3 | Day 4 | "Still haven't watched?" — direct, no guilt, specific value reminder + simple link |
| Email 4 | Day 7 (optional) | Final nudge — "The replay is still live. Here's what [patient name] said after watching it." |

### Sequence 3: Post-Webinar Sequences — Four Behavioral Paths

This is where the majority of email revenue is generated. After someone interacts with the webinar, their behavior determines which sequence they enter. Four distinct sequences, never overlapping.

**Path A — Watched + Purchased**
Exit the webinar sequence immediately. Enter the Post-Purchase Onboarding Sequence (Sequence 9). No further sales emails.

**Path B — Watched + Did Not Purchase**
*Trigger: Viewed >50% of webinar, no purchase within 24 hours*
*Length: 7 emails*
*Goal: Convert the warm but uncommitted*

This subscriber watched. They were interested enough to stay for the majority of the webinar. Something stopped them. The job of this sequence is to identify and remove that something without being pushy or repeating the same pitch.

| Email | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Day 1 | Re-hook: "Did you catch [specific moment] in the training?" — reference something specific from the webinar they watched, confirm they saw it, open a new loop |
| Email 2 | Day 2 | Epiphany story email — Secret #1 retold as a standalone story email. No pitch. Just the story and the belief shift. |
| Email 3 | Day 3 | Epiphany story email — Secret #2. Internal belief shift. The "I thought I was the problem" story. |
| Email 4 | Day 4 | Patient transformation story. Full narrative arc. The before, the wall, the discovery, the result. |
| Email 5 | Day 5 | The stack + close. Full offer recap. Subject: "Here's what's included (and the honest truth about the price)." |
| Email 6 | Day 6 | Objection handler email. Directly names the #1 reason people in this niche don't take action — and systematically takes it apart. |
| Email 7 | Day 7 | Final deadline email. Specific, legitimate urgency. What closes on this date and why. |

**Path C — Registered + Did Not Watch**
*Trigger: Registered, viewed <10% of webinar*
*Length: 5 emails*
*Goal: Get them to watch the recording*

| Email | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Day 2 | "You registered but didn't make it" — acknowledged without guilt. Replay link front and center. |
| Email 2 | Day 4 | One insight they missed. Specific. Compelling. "In the training I explain why [common thing they believe] is actually working against you. Here's a preview." |
| Email 3 | Day 6 | Secret #1 delivered as a standalone email — the full story without referencing the webinar. Ends with replay link as "want to go deeper?" |
| Email 4 | Day 8 | Secret #2 email + replay link |
| Email 5 | Day 10 | Final replay window: "The replay closes [date]. Here's what you'd be getting." Stack summary. CTA. |

**Path D — Opted In + Never Registered for Webinar**
This subscriber took the lead magnet but didn't register for the webinar. They flow into the Soap Opera Sequence (Sequence 5) for continued relationship building, with a periodic webinar invite woven in.

### Sequence 4: The Email Epiphany Funnel

**Based on:** Brunson Expert Secrets Secret #20
**Trigger:** Exhausted Path C (no-show never converted) OR used as standalone for new lists without a webinar
**Length:** 5 emails
**Goal:** Run the complete Perfect Webinar belief arc in email format — no webinar required

The Epiphany Email Writer sub-agent adapts the stories already produced by the Webinar Builder into email format. No new source content is created — the same epiphany bridges are reformatted. This means the message is consistent between the webinar and the email sequence, which reinforces rather than contradicts.

Each email ends with a cliffhanger that pulls the reader into the next one. The open rate on each subsequent email depends on the hook at the end of the previous one. This is the Soap Opera mechanic applied to the belief-building arc.

| Email | Content | Cliffhanger |
|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Origin story — the expert's backstory + the wall | "Tomorrow I'll tell you the thing I discovered that changed everything. Most practitioners never hear about it." |
| Email 2 | Secret #1 — new vehicle epiphany story | "But even after discovering this, I almost gave up. Because of one thing I believed about myself. I'll tell you what it was tomorrow." |
| Email 3 | Secret #2 — internal belief shift story | "There was still one thing standing in the way. And I bet you've told yourself the same thing. Tomorrow, I'll show you why it's not what you think." |
| Email 4 | Secret #3 — external belief shift story | "Once I understood that, only one question remained: what's the fastest, most complete way to apply this? Tomorrow I'll show you exactly what we built." |
| Email 5 | The stack + close — the offer as the logical conclusion | — |

### Sequence 5: The Soap Opera Sequence (New Subscriber Nurture)

**Based on:** Andre Chaperon's Soap Opera Sequence
**Trigger:** Any new subscriber who isn't immediately routed to the webinar (cold list, quiz funnel, organic social opt-in)
**Length:** 5–7 emails
**Goal:** Turn a cold name on a list into a warm, curious, emotionally engaged prospect who trusts the expert

Most email lists die because the first five emails are boring. The Soap Opera Sequence solves this with narrative tension — each email ends unresolved, and the next email opens on the resolution before creating a new unresolved loop.

| Email | Content | Structural role |
|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | The Dramatic Hook — opens in the middle of a compelling story moment. Not the beginning. The middle. Maximum intrigue. | Opens the loop |
| Email 2 | High Drama — the wall. The moment of maximum pain or maximum failure. The story's darkest point. | Deepens the loop |
| Email 3 | The Epiphany — the discovery moment. The turning point. Hope arrives. | Begins the resolution |
| Email 4 | The Hidden Benefits — what else became possible after the discovery. The full new world. | Expands the resolution |
| Email 5 | The Urgency + CTA — what they can do right now to start their own version of this journey | Closes + converts |

### Sequence 6: Long-Term Nurture (Ongoing Relationship)

**Trigger:** All subscribers not actively in a conversion sequence
**Cadence:** 2–3 emails per week
**Goal:** Build a relationship deep enough that the subscriber feels the expert understands them personally — and is naturally inclined to act when an offer is made

The nurture sequence is not a newsletter. It is not a content calendar. It is a rolling series of deliberately varied email types, each one selected to build a different dimension of the expert-subscriber relationship.

**Six nurture email types — rotated throughout the 90-day calendar:**

| Type | Purpose | Voice register | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Teaching email** | Valuable insight on a relevant topic. Genuinely useful. | Educator | Soft or none ("Hit reply if this resonates") |
| **Story email** | Patient transformation or personal story told as a narrative arc | Storyteller | Soft ("If you want to know how [patient] got there...") |
| **Opinion email** | A strong, polarizing take on something in the niche. The polarity content. | Conviction | None or engagement CTA ("Do you agree? Hit reply") |
| **Curation email** | "I found this and thought of you" — a study, an article, a quote, a resource | Thoughtful friend | None |
| **Bridge content email** | The sauerkraut-to-hormones type. Stretch topic with an explicit bridge back to the mechanism. | Curious and connecting | Soft (mechanism content link) |
| **Seasonal/timely email** | Holiday health angles, news hook, trending topic with the expert's perspective on it | Responsive and current | Varied |

The Nurture Email Writer produces a 90-day calendar of these emails — approximately 24–36 emails — organized so no two consecutive emails are the same type. The calendar is refreshed every 90 days.

### Sequence 7: Promotional / Launch Sequence

**Trigger:** Monthly Master Plan specifies a promotion window
**Length:** 7–10 emails over the promotional period (typically 5–7 days)
**Goal:** Convert warm list subscribers into buyers during a defined offer window

| Day | Email | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 (open) | Announcement + big promise + early-bird bonus (if applicable) | Open the window with maximum energy |
| Day 2 | Patient transformation story — emotionally resonant, full arc | Belief building through proof |
| Day 3 | Teaching email — the mechanism explained, soft CTA | Value delivery that earns the conversion |
| Day 4 | Proof email — 2–3 testimonials + results + mid-launch urgency reminder | Social proof peak |
| Day 5 | Objection handler — names the #1 reason people are hesitating and dismantles it | Remove the barrier |
| Day 6 | FAQ email — answers 5–7 most common questions about the offer | Remove remaining friction |
| Day 7 (close) AM | Morning urgency — "Today is the last day" + what closes and why | Urgency activation |
| Day 7 (close) PM | Final deadline — "Closing in [X] hours" + emotional close + final CTA | Final conversion push |

**All urgency and scarcity is legitimate only.** Price increases, enrollment windows, bonus expirations, cohort capacity limits — all documented and enforced. No fake countdown timers, no artificial scarcity. When no legitimate urgency mechanism exists, the close focuses on the cost of delay — the additional time spent without the solution.

### Sequence 8: Re-Engagement Sequence

**Trigger:** No email open in 60–90 days
**Length:** 4–5 emails
**Goal:** Re-engage genuine prospects who went cold, or cleanly remove non-responders from the active list

List hygiene is a deliverability requirement. Inactive subscribers suppress engagement rates, which damages domain reputation, which pushes active subscribers' emails into spam. Cleaning the list regularly is not optional.

| Email | Subject line style | Content | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Pattern interrupt ("We need to talk") | Light, direct: "You haven't opened an email in a while. Everything okay?" | Opens the loop |
| Email 2 | High value ("This one's worth opening") | Give them something genuinely valuable, no CTA | Re-establishes value |
| Email 3 | Direct question ("Quick question for you") | "Should I keep sending you emails about [topic]? Reply Y or N." | Engagement test |
| Email 4 | The choice ("This is the last one") | "If I don't hear from you, I'll remove you from my list. I don't want to send you things you don't want." | Final action or remove |
| Email 5 | — | Unsubscribe + move to suppression list | List cleaning |

### Sequence 10: Ascension Sequence

**Trigger:** Customer in program 30–60 days, early wins recorded, no Tier 2 purchase
**Length:** 5 emails
**Goal:** Move Tier 1 buyers to Tier 2 or higher naturally — as the logical next step from where they are

This sequence is a mini Email Epiphany Funnel built around the transformation that becomes possible at the next tier. It is not a hard sell. It is a vision sequence: "Here's what opens up once you've done what you've already done."

Structure mirrors the Soap Opera Sequence — each email advances the vision of what's possible at the next level, ends with a cliffhanger, and closes with a clear offer on Email 5.

## SMS Sequences

SMS is a distinct channel with distinct rules. It does not replace email. It amplifies at critical moments where immediacy matters and email open rates are insufficient.

**What SMS is for:**
- Webinar show-rate improvement (critical window reminders)
- Assessment/appointment reminders (no-show prevention)
- Promotional deadline amplification (final hours of an offer window)
- Cart abandonment (same-day, while purchase intent is highest)
- Re-engagement pings (cross-channel: "Did you see my email about X?")

**What SMS is never for:**
- Regular nurture or teaching content
- Anything that requires more than 160 characters to say properly
- Any send where the subscriber hasn't explicitly opted in to SMS

### SMS Sequences Built

| Sequence | Messages | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| **Webinar show-rate** | 3 SMS | 24h before, 1h before, 5min before |
| **Cart abandonment** | 2 SMS | Same day (4h after abandon), next day |
| **Assessment reminder** | 3 SMS | Booking confirmation, 24h before, 1h before |
| **Promotional deadline** | 3 SMS | 48h before close, day-of morning, final 2 hours |
| **Re-engagement ping** | 1 SMS | When re-engagement email sequence starts |

### SMS Compliance Framework

SMS marketing is governed by jurisdiction-specific law. The clinicIQ SMS Builder enforces compliance automatically based on the expert's primary market:

| Jurisdiction | Law | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| United States | TCPA | Express written consent before any SMS; opt-out honored within 10 days; sender identification in every message |
| Canada | CASL | Commercial messages require express or implied consent; unsubscribe mechanism required |
| European Union | GDPR + ePrivacy | Explicit opt-in required; right to erasure honored; documentation of consent |
| United Kingdom | ICO/PECR | Similar to EU; explicit consent required |

**The clinicIQ standard:** Consent is captured at opt-in and explicitly documented. SMS opt-in is separate from email opt-in — not assumed. The SMS Sequence Writer checks every message for: opt-out path present, sender identified, timing compliance (no messages before 8am or after 9pm local time), character count within limits.

## Email Deliverability Standards

Deliverability is not a technical detail — it is the foundational requirement. An email that goes to spam is an email that doesn't exist. The entire sequence architecture fails if the emails don't land.

### The Seven Deliverability Factors

**1. Domain reputation**
New domains must be warmed up. Mass sending on a cold domain is the fastest way to get blacklisted. Warm-up schedule: 50/day for week 1, 100/day week 2, 250/day week 3, scaling from there. The Deliverability Auditor monitors domain reputation scores.

**2. List hygiene**
Inactive subscribers suppress engagement rates. Gmail and Outlook track per-domain open and click rates. A list with 30% inactive subscribers is dragging the other 70% into spam. The Re-engagement Sequence + suppression cycle is not optional — it is a deliverability maintenance requirement built into the Monthly Master Plan.

**3. Authentication**
Every expert's email domain must have DKIM, SPF, and DMARC records correctly configured before any sequences go live. The Email Builder produces a one-time configuration guide for the expert's specific platform and domain registrar. Without this, emails will fail authentication checks and be filtered or rejected by major providers.

**4. Spam trigger avoidance**
The Deliverability Auditor scans every email for: spam trigger words in subject lines and first lines, excessive use of caps or exclamation marks, link mismatches (displayed URL different from actual URL), image-heavy templates with little text, missing physical address (CAN-SPAM requirement), and missing unsubscribe link.

**5. Text-to-image ratio**
Heavy image emails trigger spam filters on Gmail and Outlook. The Email Builder defaults to text-primary formatting for all sequences. Brand headers and footers use minimal image weight. If a visual element is included, it is used purposefully — not as decoration.

**6. Engagement signals**
Send to your most engaged subscribers first. A high early open rate signals positive sender reputation for the full send. The Segmentation Logic Builder segments sends by engagement tier.

**7. Unsubscribe compliance**
One-click unsubscribe is required in every email. Requests are honored within 10 days (CAN-SPAM) or immediately (best practice). The Email Builder includes the unsubscribe link as a non-removable element in every email template.

## Email Copy Craft Standards

### Subject Lines

The subject line is the most important creative decision in email marketing. A strong subject line gets the email opened. Everything else is irrelevant if the subject is weak.

**Seven subject line types for health audiences:**

| Type | Example | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Curiosity** | "The hormone test your doctor isn't running" | Nurture, cold sequences |
| **Specificity + benefit** | "How [patient name] reversed [condition] in 47 days" | Proof sequences |
| **Pattern interrupt** | "I was wrong about this" | Re-engagement, cold list |
| **Question** | "Are you making this mistake?" | Awareness level 2–3 |
| **Number** | "3 signs your thyroid is struggling (even with normal labs)" | Teaching, content |
| **Story tease** | "She was told it was just anxiety. It wasn't." | Soap opera, epiphany |
| **Controversy** | "Why I stopped recommending [common supplement]" | Opinion, polarity |
| **Direct / Transactional** | "Your replay is live (but not for long)" | Promotional, urgency |

The Subject Line Specialist produces 3–5 variants for every email in every sequence. A/B test pairs are produced for all high-stakes emails (launch sequences, epiphany funnel emails, post-webinar re-pitch).

**Preview text rules:**
- Extends the subject line hook OR adds a second curiosity hook
- Never repeats the subject line verbatim
- Never defaults to "View this email in your browser"
- Never blank (blank preview text defaults to the first line of the email — waste of prime real estate)

### Email Body Structure

**The opening hook** — the first 1–2 sentences determine whether the email is read or closed. They must create enough tension, curiosity, or emotional resonance that skipping forward or closing would feel like a loss.

Good openers for health audiences:
- Starting mid-story: "She'd been told her labs were normal for the third time in two years."
- A counterintuitive statement: "The supplement most hormone patients are taking is making their problem worse."
- A direct question: "Have you ever felt like your doctor understands the numbers but not you?"
- A specific detail: "It was 3am when the message came in. Another patient who couldn't sleep."

**Paragraph length:** 1–3 sentences maximum. White space accelerates reading. Long blocks of text feel like work.

**The PS line:** The most-read element in any email after the subject line. Never waste it on legal text or social links. Use it as a second hook, a secondary CTA, a teaser for the next email, or a one-line proof statement.

**CTA placement and structure:**
- One CTA per email (exception: re-engagement emails with binary choice)
- The CTA comes after the value has been delivered — not before
- Specific action language: "Watch the training" not "Click here"
- Link appears as plain hyperlinked text in most sequence emails (not a button) — more personal, better deliverability
- Buttons acceptable for transactional emails (purchase confirmation, access delivery)

**Email length targets:**

| Email Type | Target Length | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Nurture / story | 350–600 words | Story needs room; too short feels incomplete |
| Teaching | 400–800 words | Depth earns trust; too long loses momentum |
| Promotional | 200–400 words | Get to the point; they know what they're there for |
| Epiphany sequence | 600–1,000 words | The belief shift requires the full story arc |
| SMS | ≤160 characters | Channel constraint; never compromise clarity for brevity |

The correct length is: the length at which the last word is necessary. Not the length that feels impressive or the length that fits a template.

## Behavioral Segmentation Architecture

The email system is not a single list getting the same messages. It is a behavioral graph — different subscribers receive different sequences based on what they've done.

**The four primary segment dimensions:**

| Dimension | Values | How it's captured |
|---|---|---|
| Opt-in source | Lead magnet type, quiz result, content opt-in | Form tag at opt-in |
| Awareness level | 1–5 | Quiz result or inferred from opt-in source |
| Webinar behavior | Not registered / Registered / Watched <10% / Watched >50% / Purchased | Video platform + payment webhook |
| Purchase status | No purchase / Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3 | Payment platform webhook |

**Sequence enrollment rules (priority order):**
1. Post-purchase sequences always override everything else
2. Active promotional deadline sequences override nurture
3. Re-engagement sequences pause nurture temporarily
4. Webinar follow-up sequences override the general welcome sequence once triggered
5. Nurture runs as the background state for all subscribers not in any priority sequence

**The Segmentation Logic Builder** produces the complete if/then automation logic for the expert's chosen platform (ActiveCampaign, GoHighLevel, Klaviyo, ConvertKit). This logic is platform-ready — formatted for direct import into the automation builder.

## The Reply Handling System

Health audiences reply to emails. Not occasionally — frequently. Personal health stories, questions about protocols, requests for specific guidance, expressions of gratitude that open into buying conversations.

These replies are a feature, not an overhead problem. A health expert who replies to emails creates relationship depth that no automation can replicate. But replies also require templates — because the same questions arrive repeatedly.

The Email Builder produces a **Reply Template Library** for every expert:
- The 10 most common question types for this expert's specialty
- Each template: a genuine, personal-feeling response that answers the question fully, maintains the expert's voice, and where appropriate, routes to the relevant program or resource
- Structured as starting points, not scripts — the expert personalizes before sending

**Human-in-the-loop requirement:** Reply handling is not automated. These are real conversations that require a real human (the expert or a trained team member) to read and respond. clinicIQ provides the templates. The expert provides the care.

## Three System-Level Additions

### 1. Always-On Email Production — One-Offs, New Series, New Promotionals

The Email Builder is not a one-time setup agent. It is a standing production system. At any point, the expert can request:

- **One-off emails** — "I want to send an email about [topic] today." Quick-Create mode. Lead Agent parses the request, auto-fills Voice DNA and context from the dossier, produces a complete email (subject variants, preview, body, PS) in minutes.
- **New promotional sequences** — "We're running a sale on [offer] for 5 days." The Promotional Email Writer builds the full launch arc on demand.
- **New email series** — "I want a 7-part series on [topic]." The appropriate sub-agent (Nurture Writer, Epiphany Writer, or Soap Opera Writer depending on the goal) builds it as a standalone series that can be inserted into the sequence library or sent as a broadcast series.
- **Additional nurture batches** — The 90-day calendar refreshes automatically every 90 days. The Nurture Email Writer generates the next block without being asked, based on the current hook bank and Monthly Master Plan.

The expert should never hit a point where they've "used up" their email content. The system is designed to produce more on request and proactively.

### 2. Performance Measurement, Iteration, and Replacement

Every email in every sequence is measured. Every metric feeds back into the system. Underperforming emails are replaced, not just flagged.

**Metrics tracked per email (fed from the email platform via analytics integration):**

| Metric | What it signals | Action threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Open rate | Subject line performance | <15% open rate = subject line replaced |
| Click rate | Copy and CTA effectiveness | <2% CTR = body copy and CTA reviewed |
| Click-to-open rate (CTOR) | Body quality independent of subject | <10% CTOR = body rewrite triggered |
| Unsubscribe rate | Relevance and frequency | >0.5% on any single email = flag for review |
| Reply rate | Emotional resonance | Tracked as a positive signal; high reply = story email worth amplifying |
| Conversion rate | Sequence-level purchase attribution | Tracked per sequence, not just per email |

**The optimization loop:**

The Performance Learning Agent (Layer 4) receives these metrics and routes them to the Email Builder for action:

- **Subject line underperforming** → Subject Line Specialist produces 3 new variants from untested types → A/B test runs → winner replaces the original
- **Body/CTA underperforming** → The responsible sub-agent rewrites that specific email with the failure reason as context → replacement goes live
- **Full sequence underperforming** → Sequence Strategy Agent reviews the arc → identifies the weak link (usually the transition between emails or a story that doesn't land) → targeted rewrite
- **Emails that are simply old** → The system flags any nurture email that has been in the rotation for more than 6 months → Nurture Writer produces a replacement using more recent angles from the hook bank

Nothing in the email library is permanent. Everything is measured. Anything below threshold gets replaced.

### 3. Never-Stop Follow-Up — The Non-Buyer Engine

If someone opted in and hasn't bought, the system never stops working on them — until they buy or they unsubscribe. There is no "we gave up on this lead."

**How the never-stop engine works:**

After the formal post-webinar sequence ends and no purchase has occurred, the subscriber does not simply fall into passive nurture. They enter a **rolling non-buyer conversion track** — a continuously refreshing sequence of:

1. **Fresh story emails** — new patient transformation stories they haven't seen, drawn from the Proof Bank as it grows
2. **New angle emails** — different hook angles on the same mechanism (hook bank grows every month; new angles = new chances to click)
3. **Lead magnet value emails** — every lead magnet the expert has ever created becomes a free email-deliverable value piece. If they didn't buy the hormone program, they get "here's a free guide to [related topic]" — which re-warms them, demonstrates value, and opens a new conversion window. The lead magnet is not just a top-of-funnel tool — it is a mid-funnel re-engagement weapon.
4. **Periodic re-pitch windows** — every 30–45 days, a non-buyer who is still on the list and opening emails enters a short (3-email) re-pitch sequence tied to a legitimate event (new testimonial added, price change, new bonus, new cohort opening, relevant news hook)
5. **Content-driven micro-conversions** — every email in the non-buyer track has a soft conversion path: a content piece, a free resource, a quiz, a short video — small actions that re-engage the subscriber's investment in the expert's world without a hard sell

**The unsubscribe is the only exit.** A subscriber who unsubscribes has self-selected out — they made a clear choice, it's honored immediately, and the list is cleaner for it. But until that choice is made, the system continues to show up with value, stories, new angles, and periodic offers.

**Lead magnets as email value:**
Every lead magnet in the expert's library is tagged in the Asset Library as an email-deliverable value piece. The Nurture Email Writer has access to this library and uses lead magnets as:
- Standalone value emails ("I made this guide a while back — thought you'd want it")
- Re-engagement tools for cold segments
- Mid-funnel proof of expertise before a re-pitch
- Bridge content (a lead magnet on a stretch topic is the perfect bridge email)

The lead magnet library grows over time. Each new one — produced by the Lead Magnet Builder (Step 15) — is automatically available to the Email Builder as new email content.
