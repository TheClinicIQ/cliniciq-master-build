# ClinicIQ — Avatar Army: Synthetic Expert Testing System
## Thousands of simulated health experts to stress-test every path through the platform

---

## THE SYSTEM

Each synthetic expert is a fully-realized persona with: a name, credentials, specialty, sub-niche, practice model, experience level, personality type, communication style, answer quality tier, a patient avatar they serve, a mechanism they use, an origin story, proof assets, and an offer. They go through onboarding, answer IQ's questions in-character, interact with builders and engines, and produce outputs we can evaluate.

The goal: find every failure mode before real experts do. Thin answers that produce generic content. Edge cases that break builders. Specialties where the expander prompts miss. Personality types that IQ handles poorly. Answer quality levels where the platform produces garbage vs. gold.

## TIER 2: ANSWER QUALITY TIERS (5 levels)

Each synthetic expert gets assigned a quality tier that determines how they respond to IQ's questions.

### Tier A — Gold Responder (10% of avatars)
**Who they are:** Experienced, articulate, has done marketing work before. Knows their positioning. Has stories ready. Speaks in specifics.
**How they answer:** Long, detailed, specific. Names patients. Gives timelines. Uses vivid language. Has a clear mechanism. Knows their contrarian position. Origin story is emotional and detailed.
**Example Q2 answer:** "The first thing I do is run a full organic acids test and a comprehensive thyroid panel — not just TSH, but free T3, free T4, reverse T3, TPO and TG antibodies. Most doctors run TSH only and call it normal. I've had patients come in with a TSH of 2.5 and a reverse T3 through the roof — their body was converting T4 to the wrong form. Nobody caught it because nobody looked."
**Tests:** Whether IQ can handle abundance — does it capture everything? Does the expander produce an elite profile?

### Tier B — Solid Responder (25% of avatars)
**Who they are:** Competent practitioner, clear about their work, but hasn't articulated it for marketing before. Moderate detail. Gets to the point but doesn't volunteer extras.
**How they answer:** Clear but not elaborate. Answers the question directly. Doesn't go deep on emotion or story unless pushed.
**Example Q2 answer:** "I run comprehensive bloodwork including thyroid and hormone panels. Most patients come to me after their regular doctor missed something. I look at functional ranges, not just conventional."
**Tests:** Whether IQ's follow-up probing can extract more depth. Whether the expander can build a full profile from moderate input.

### Tier C — Surface Responder (30% of avatars)
**Who they are:** Knows their clinical work cold but can't articulate what makes them different. Gives clinical/technical answers when asked about positioning. Struggles with marketing-oriented questions.
**How they answer:** Short, clinical, jargon-heavy. Answers about patients in diagnoses not human terms. Origin story is "I've always been interested in health."
**Example Q2 answer:** "I take a functional approach looking at root causes including gut health, hormones, and detoxification pathways."
**Tests:** Whether IQ's weak response protocol works — does it expand this? Do the option trees help? Does the expander produce something usable or generic?

### Tier D — Struggling Responder (25% of avatars)
**Who they are:** New practitioner, or experienced but introverted/uncertain. Gives minimal answers. Says "I don't know" to some questions. Uncomfortable with marketing language.
**How they answer:** Very short. Lots of "I guess" and "I'm not sure." May defer: "I just help people." Some questions get blank responses.
**Example Q2 answer:** "I mean, I do testing. Like blood work and stuff. I try to look at the whole picture."
**Tests:** Whether IQ's Level 3 (option tree) and Level 4 (AI generates) work. Whether the coaching moments fire correctly. Whether the expander can build something usable from almost nothing.

### Tier E — Resistant/Difficult Responder (10% of avatars)
**Who they are:** Skeptical of the platform. Gives pushback. Challenges IQ. May be hostile to marketing language. Or: wildly verbose and goes off on tangents. Or: contradicts themselves.
**How they answer:** Unpredictable. May refuse questions. May give 500 words when 50 would do. May say one thing about their mechanism and then contradict it later. May challenge IQ: "Why do you need to know that?" May be dismissive: "Just make me some content."
**Example Q2 answer:** "Look, I'm not going to sit here and explain my whole methodology. I've been doing this for 20 years. My patients get better. That should be enough."
**Tests:** Whether IQ handles resistance with grace. Whether the pushback dial works. Whether the platform can produce anything useful from hostile input.

## TIER 3: PERSONALITY TYPES (crossed with quality tiers)

### P1 — The Confident Expert
Declarative. Direct. Doesn't hedge. "This is what I do and it works." Easy to capture voice DNA. May skip IQ's expansion and just approve.

### P2 — The Academic
Long explanations. Citations. Loves mechanism depth. May over-explain. Risk: content sounds like a journal article, not marketing.

### P3 — The Healer
Warm. Emotional. Patient-centered. Talks about transformation, not protocols. Great story material. Risk: may resist "marketing" framing.

### P4 — The Entrepreneur
Business-minded. Asks about ROI. Wants to see the offer stack. Impatient with "tell me about your feelings" questions. Moves fast.

### P5 — The Reluctant Marketer
Great clinician, hates marketing. "I just want to help people." Needs coaching on why positioning matters. May give weak answers out of discomfort, not ignorance.

### P6 — The Multi-Hyphenate
Does 5 things. Treats everyone. Has 3 offers. Can't pick one avatar. Needs the "I help everyone" coaching moment. Tests IQ's ability to narrow.

### P7 — The Brand-New Practitioner
Just starting. No proof. No offer. No patient stories. Has passion but no track record. Tests IQ's ability to build from almost nothing.

## TIER 4: PRACTICE MODELS (crossed with everything above)

### M1 — Solo Telehealth
Just them. National reach. Webcam visits. Usually functional/integrative.

### M2 — Solo In-Office
Local practice. One location. Sees patients in person.

### M3 — Group Practice
Multiple practitioners. They're the lead/founder. Need to position the practice, not just themselves.

### M4 — Hybrid (Telehealth + In-Office)
Both virtual and in-person. Geographic + national reach.

### M5 — Cash-Pay Only
No insurance. Higher prices. Needs to justify the investment.

### M6 — Insurance + Cash Hybrid
Takes some insurance, has cash-pay premium services.

### M7 — Program-Based (Not Visit-Based)
Sells programs (12-week, 6-month, etc.) not individual visits.

### M8 — Course/Digital Product
Sells courses, digital programs, memberships. May not see patients 1:1.

### M9 — High-Ticket Concierge / VIP
$10K+ programs. Small patient volume. Premium positioning.

## RECOMMENDED AVATAR COUNT FOR THOROUGH TESTING

### Phase 1 — Core Coverage (200 avatars)
Every sub-niche × every quality tier × at least 1 personality + practice model variation.
- 14 categories × ~8 sub-niches = ~112 sub-niches
- Each sub-niche gets at least 1 avatar at Tier C (the most common real user) + 1 at Tier D (the hardest to handle)
- ~200 avatars covers the category × sub-niche × quality matrix

### Phase 2 — Edge Case Stress Test (100 avatars)
Specifically designed to break things:
- 20 Tier E (resistant/difficult) across diverse categories
- 20 P6 (multi-hyphenate — "I help everyone") across quality tiers
- 20 P7 (brand-new — no proof, no offer, no stories) across categories
- 20 with contradictory answers (say one thing about mechanism, different thing about patients)
- 20 with extremely niche sub-specialties (pediatric holistic dentistry, vestibular therapy, compounding pharmacy) to test whether the expander handles uncommon specialties

### Phase 3 — Full Scale (1,000+ avatars)
Randomized combinations across the full matrix. Automated runs through onboarding + at least 1 engine + content generation. Outputs scored.

**Total: Start with 200. Expand to 300. Then 1,000+.**

## HOW TO USE THESE AVATARS

### For Onboarding Testing:
Load the avatar as the system prompt for a test agent. The test agent goes through IQ's onboarding and answers in-character, using the pre-written responses for that avatar's quality tier. Evaluate:
- Did IQ's probing extract enough depth?
- Did the expander produce a usable profile?
- Were the 3 false beliefs specific to this avatar, or generic?
- Did Voice DNA capture real signal, or default to generic?
- Did the lead magnet options make sense for this specialty?

### For Builder Testing:
After onboarding produces a dossier, feed it to each builder:
- Content Builder: does it produce posts that sound like THIS expert?
- Webinar Builder: do the 3 secrets break THIS avatar's 3 false beliefs?
- Email Builder: does the nurture sequence address THIS avatar's objections?
- Ad Creative: do the hooks use THIS avatar's failure language?
- Funnel Builder: does the order page speak to THIS avatar's fear and desire?
- Lead Magnet Builder: does it produce something THIS avatar would actually use?

### For Engine Testing:
Trigger a Growth Engine (e.g., Evergreen Webinar Funnel) from the dossier. Evaluate:
- Does the webinar script use the expert's origin story?
- Does the email sequence target the right false beliefs?
- Do the ad creatives use the right hook angles?
- Does the funnel page speak to the right avatar?
- Is everything in the expert's voice?

### Scoring:
Each output gets scored on:
- **Voice match** (0-100): does it sound like THIS expert?
- **Avatar accuracy** (0-100): does it target THIS avatar's psychology?
- **Mechanism usage** (0-100): does it use the expert's mechanism correctly?
- **Strategic coherence** (0-100): does it align with the Brand North Star?
- **Conversion readiness** (0-100): would a real patient engage with this?

Outputs scoring below 70 on any dimension flag for investigation. The avatar spec + the output + the dossier become a test case for prompt improvement.

## GENERATION APPROACH

### For Phase 1 (200 avatars):
Write the first 20 avatar specs by hand (like the Dr. Sarah Mitchell example above). These become the gold standard. Then use an LLM to generate the remaining 180 from the matrix, using the hand-written 20 as few-shot examples. Each generated avatar must include:
- Full persona details
- Pre-written answers for all 23+ onboarding questions at their quality tier
- Specialty-specific mechanism, avatar, and proof
- Realistic credentials for their category

### For Phase 2 (100 edge cases):
Hand-design 20 specific failure scenarios. Generate the remaining 80 as variations. These are designed to break the system — contradictory answers, hostile personalities, blank responses, wildly verbose tangents, niche specialties the expander may not know.

### For Phase 3 (1,000+):
Fully automated generation from the matrix. Each avatar gets a random combination of category × sub-niche × quality tier × personality × practice model. Answers are generated by an LLM playing that persona. No human review needed at this scale — the scoring system catches quality issues.
