# ClinicIQ — Marketing Synthetic Reviewers
## A panel of AI experts modeled after the best marketers, copywriters, and conversion specialists alive or ever to have lived. They review platform output at scale so you don't have to.

---

## THE PRINCIPLE

You can't review every piece of output from 100+ synthetic expert accounts running through the platform. But you CAN review the outputs that a panel of world-class AI marketing synthetics flags as problematic, mediocre, or exceptional.

Each synthetic is a persona built from a real marketer's methodology, writing, interviews, frameworks, and public work. They review outputs through their specific lens. One output might get scored by 5+ synthetics depending on what it is — a VSL gets reviewed by the copywriting synthetic, the webinar synthetic, the conversion synthetic, and the offer synthetic.

Each synthetic outputs:
- **Score** (0-100) on their domain
- **Strengths** — what's working
- **Critical issues** — what's broken that must be fixed
- **Suggestions** — what would make it stronger
- **Verdict** — SHIP / FIX / REWRITE

When multiple synthetics flag the same issue, it's a real problem. When they all give high scores, you can trust the output.

**S_UX05 — The End-to-End Journey Synthetic ("The User Simulator")**
This is the big one. Not modeled after a specific person — modeled after the practice of literally walking through the platform as a new user from signup to first engine deployed.

**What it does:** Runs a full simulated journey as an avatar would experience it:
1. Lands on marketing site → does CTA make sense?
2. Signs up → does confirmation flow work? Email delivery?
3. First login → does onboarding start immediately? Is IQ personable?
4. Through all 6 onboarding sections → any friction? Confusion? Dropout points?
5. Lead magnet selection → are options clear? Is the build expectation set?
6. Lands in the platform post-onboarding → do they know what to do next?
7. Tries to review their first engine → can they find it? Approve it? Edit it?
8. Tries to access their strategy documents → where are they? Clear?
9. Tries to make a change → can they update their voice? Their avatar?
10. Wants to add a new engine → clear path?
11. Wants to understand their ROI → is there a dashboard?
12. Wants help → is there support? Docs?

**Lens:** "I am a new user. I know nothing. I just want to [outcome]. Can I do it? Where did I get stuck?"

**Frameworks they apply:** Jobs-to-be-Done, Customer Journey Mapping, Heuristic Evaluation at every step
**Strongest at:** Finding the disconnects between stages — the gaps where the platform assumes context the user doesn't have
**What they catch:** Broken handoffs between systems, orphaned flows, missing "what now?" moments, unclear ownership (where does this live?), context loss when moving between sections

**Every synthetic expert avatar's journey through the platform also gets reviewed by this synthetic.** Not just "did the output look good" but "was the journey to get there coherent?"

### DOMAIN 14: TEMPLATE DETECTION

**S_TD01 — The Voice Uniqueness Synthetic**
Modeled after: A composite of voice-of-customer research, AI-generated content detection methodology, and brand voice analysts. Not a real person — purpose-built to catch the #1 failure mode of AI marketing.

**What they look for:**
- Template phrases: "wellness journey," "holistic approach," "empowered to heal," "root cause solutions," "transform your life," "unlock your potential," "reclaim your health," "on your journey," "sacred space," "deeply nourishing," "life-changing," "game-changing," "natural path to wellness" — the AI health content sludge vocabulary
- Template structures: "In this article, we'll explore..." / "Many people ask..." / "But here's the thing..." / rhetorical questions stacked / three-sentence paragraphs with no variation / "X is more than just Y" openers
- Sentence rhythm uniformity — AI tends to write sentences of similar length; real humans vary dramatically

- Missing voice specificity — no specific patient stories, no specific clinical details, no specific numbers, no specific frustrations

## MULTI-SYNTHETIC CONSENSUS

When an output is reviewed by multiple synthetics, the system aggregates:

**Flagged for rewrite if:**
- 2+ synthetics score below 60
- Any synthetic flags a compliance/legal issue (S29)
- Average panel score below 70

**Flagged for fix if:**
- Average score is 65-75
- Specific critical issues cited but no rewrite needed

**Cleared to ship if:**
- All synthetics score 80+
- No critical issues flagged
- S29 compliance passed

**Exceptional output (study these):**
- All synthetics score 90+
- At least one synthetic names it as best-in-class
- Flag for human review → feed back into system as training example

## HOW YOU USE THIS

1. **100 synthetic experts run through ClinicIQ.** Their outputs stack up — thousands of emails, ads, webinar scripts, funnel pages, social posts, lead magnets.

2. **The marketing synthetics auto-review everything.** Each output gets 3-5 reviews from the relevant panel.

3. **Dashboard shows you only what matters:**
   - Red: Outputs flagged for rewrite. These are broken. Review the pattern — is it the expert avatar? The prompt? The builder?
   - Yellow: Outputs with specific fixes needed. These are usable with tweaks.
   - Green: Outputs cleared to ship. Trust them.
   - Gold star: Outputs multiple synthetics praised. Study these — they reveal what's working.

4. **You personally review:** all the Golds (maybe 2%), a sample of Reds (find patterns), and ~10% of Yellows (spot-check).

5. **The system learns.** Patterns in reviews feed back into prompt tuning. If S13 Fladlien consistently flags the same missing element across webinar outputs, that's a prompt fix, not a per-output fix.
