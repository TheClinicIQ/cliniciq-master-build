# ClinicIQ Ad Hooks Strategy
## Full Framework: Scroll-Stopping Hooks → Burning Questions → VSL Alignment

---

## THE STRATEGIC OVERVIEW

The job of the hook is NOT to explain ClinicIQ.

The job of the hook is to **stop a specific person** (the right health expert), **plant a burning question they must have answered**, and **drive them into the VSL where that question gets answered** — creating a satisfying, pre-sold emotional arc by the time they hit the offer.

The hook and the VSL work as one mechanism. Hook opens the loop. VSL closes it.

---

## STEP 1: WHO ARE WE STOPPING? (The Targeting Filter Built Into the Hook)

Before writing a single hook, define the exact person the hook must filter for. The ad platform will find anyone. The hook must qualify them.

**The right health expert has ALL of these:**

| Signal | They are IN | They are OUT |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Identity | Doctor, practitioner, coach, functional medicine, health expert | Consumer looking for health advice |
| Awareness | Has tried to grow their practice online | Just starting in health |
| Pain | Frustrated with inconsistent results despite effort | Happy with their current growth |
| Ambition | Wants to scale, reach more people, build something | Wants to stay small / just practice medicine |
| Capacity | Can invest in infrastructure | Bootstrapped, no budget mindset |
| Tech posture | Not anti-AI, but not yet agentic | Already using full agentic systems |

**The hook must speak to the frustrated, ambitious, growth-oriented health expert who has tried things and hit a wall.**

This is the entire creative filter. Every word of the hook runs through: *"Would the right person recognize themselves in this?"*

---

## STEP 2: THE 5 AWARENESS LEVELS AND WHAT BURNING QUESTION EACH ONE NEEDS

The ads document already maps to the 5 levels of awareness. Here is each level's **strategic job**, the **emotional state** of the viewer, and the **burning question we want to plant**.

---

### LEVEL 1: UNAWARE
**Their emotional state:** Content, or vaguely stressed about the business — but not connecting it to anything specific. They don't know agentic AI exists or matters.

**Strategic job of the hook:** Create a pattern interrupt. Make them realize their business model has a problem they haven't seen yet. Do NOT mention AI. Do NOT mention ClinicIQ. Meet them at their actual life.

**The burning question we want them entering the VSL with:**
> *"What is this shift happening in my market that I don't know about — and how far behind am I?"*

**Secondary desire to activate:** Fear of being left behind by something they can't see yet + curiosity about what the "first type of practice" is doing differently.

**Alignment with VSL:** The VSL opens with exactly this — "if everything you've tried starts to work and then stops working — that is not bad luck — that is a systems problem." The unaware person arrives asking "what is happening?" and the VSL answers it immediately and viscerally.

---

### LEVEL 2: PROBLEM AWARE
**Their emotional state:** Frustrated. Knows something is broken. Has tried things. Can't figure out the actual root cause. Oscillates between blaming the market, blaming themselves, and blaming whatever they most recently tried.

**Strategic job of the hook:** Name their specific pain so accurately they feel seen. Do NOT present the solution yet. Make them desperately want to know: is there actually a fix for this?

**The burning question we want them entering the VSL with:**
> *"Is there a system that actually fixes ALL of this — not just patches one piece of it?"*

**Secondary desire to activate:** Relief that someone finally diagnosed the real problem + hope that the fix is real and specific.

**Alignment with VSL:** The VSL's first 40% is entirely the problem — three problems layered on top of each other: the numbers problem, the team problem, and the "sick patient doesn't buy like a normal consumer" problem. The problem-aware person arrives wanting diagnosis, and the VSL delivers the deepest, most specific diagnosis they've ever heard about their exact situation.

---

### LEVEL 3: SOLUTION AWARE
**Their emotional state:** "I use AI. I'm on top of this." Slightly skeptical. Self-satisfied. They think they've already solved it with ChatGPT or whatever tool they're using.

**Strategic job of the hook:** Disrupt their comfort. Create cognitive dissonance between "I use AI" and "you're not actually AI-native." The hook must make them question whether what they have is a real strategy or just a faster manual workflow.

**The burning question we want them entering the VSL with:**
> *"What's the actual difference between what I'm doing and what a real AI-native business looks like — and why does it matter?"*

**Secondary desire to activate:** The fear that they're behind while thinking they're ahead is viscerally uncomfortable. Curiosity about the five-layer stack.

**Alignment with VSL:** The VSL's "what an agent actually is" vs. "ChatGPT is a tool, an agent is a department head" section answers this perfectly. The solution-aware person arrives with the specific question "how is this different?" and the VSL makes that gap feel enormous.

---

### LEVEL 4: PRODUCT AWARE
**Their emotional state:** Knows Dr. Axe. Has likely seen content. Respects the credibility. But hasn't pulled the trigger. Some mix of: "is this right for me?" / "can I afford it?" / "is now the right time?"

**Strategic job of the hook:** Lead with the full weight of the mission and credentials. No education needed. Make them feel the gravity of the opportunity and the cost of not moving. This is conviction, not explanation.

**The burning question we want them entering the VSL with:**
> *"What exactly is this — and what would it look like for my specific practice?"*

**Secondary desire to activate:** FOMO. The window is real and closing. The people moving now compound before it's obvious.

**Alignment with VSL:** The VSL's offer section, the call to action, and the "humans in the loop" section answer the product-aware person's "what is it exactly and can I trust it?" directly. They arrive pre-sold on the person; the VSL sells the system.

---

### LEVEL 5: MOST AWARE
**Their emotional state:** Has seen everything. Understands the gap. Just hasn't moved. Procrastination masquerading as evaluation.

**Strategic job of the hook:** No more education. Pure urgency and identity pressure. The cost of inaction needs to feel real and imminent. This hook is almost an intervention.

**The burning question we want them entering the VSL with:**
> *"If I keep waiting, what exactly am I giving up — and when does the window close?"*

**Secondary desire to activate:** Shame of inaction converted to forward momentum. Make the decision feel obvious and overdue.

**Alignment with VSL:** The VSL close — "the biggest opportunities don't announce themselves loudly, they show up quietly, and the people who pay attention and move early are the ones who look back and say — that was the moment everything changed" — is exactly the answer. They arrive asking "should I move now?" and the VSL ends with a direct, quiet conviction that answers yes.

## STEP 3: THE HOOKS — COMPLETE LIBRARY BY AWARENESS LEVEL

Each hook is written in two formats:
- **Text overlay** (for video scroll-stop — 5-10 words, max impact)
- **Verbal/VO hook** (what's spoken in the first 3 seconds of the video)

The text overlay and verbal hook should work together — the text stops the scroll, the voice pulls them in.

## STEP 4: THE COMPLETE HOOK SELECTION PROCESS

**How to get to the best possible hooks from this library:**

### Phase 1 — Creative Selection (Right Now)
1. **Match the ad to the awareness level of the audience segment you're targeting.** Cold traffic = UA/PA. Warm retargeting (watched content before) = SA/PR/MA.
2. **Pick 2–3 hooks per awareness level** for the first round. These are your test candidates.
3. **Evaluate each hook against the filter:** Would a frustrated, ambitious health expert who has tried things and hit a wall stop scrolling for this? If yes → test it. If it could attract someone who just wants health tips → cut it.

### Phase 2 — Testing (Weeks 1–4)
- Run each hook as a separate creative variant
- **Primary metric to watch: Thumb Stop Rate** (percentage who watch past 3 seconds) — this is the pure hook performance metric
- **Secondary metric: View-to-click rate** — this measures whether the hook attracted the RIGHT person (quality, not just volume)
- **Third metric: Opt-in quality** — are the leads who come in health experts with practices, or are they consumers? Survey them on the opt-in page.

### Phase 3 — Iteration (Weeks 5+)
- Take the top 2 performing hooks by Thumb Stop Rate
- Write 5 variations of each, changing only: the first 3 words / the emotional tone / the specific pain named
- Re-run. Identify the winning variation.
- Identify which awareness level is converting best end-to-end (hook → opt-in → VSL watch time → booked call) and weight spend accordingly.

## STEP 5: ADDITIONAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR FULL ALIGNMENT

### The Continuity Rule
The hook opens a specific loop. The VSL must close that specific loop — and do it before the 30% mark. If the loop opened is "what is happening in my market?" — the VSL must address market disruption early. If the loop opened is "what are the 5 numbers?" — the VSL must deliver those numbers with real depth.

**For each hook in production, document:**
- What question does this hook plant?
- At what timestamp in the VSL does that question get answered?
- Is the answer satisfying, or does it feel like a pivot to a different subject?

Any hook whose question isn't answered by the VSL within the first 5–7 minutes needs to be cut or the VSL needs to be updated.

### The Identity Test
Before running any hook, run this check:
> *"If a successful health expert who is already doing well sees this — would they feel this is for them, or would they feel like this is for someone struggling?"*

The best hooks speak to ambitious people, not broken people. Pain-based hooks should frame the problem as a structural gap, not a personal failure. "You don't have a marketing problem, you have an infrastructure problem" is respectful. "Why are you still doing everything manually?" is slightly shaming. The first converts. The second generates bad feelings at scroll-stop.

### The Curiosity Gap Rule
Every hook must create a specific, answerable question — not general curiosity. 

❌ "AI is changing health." (Too vague — no specific question formed)  
✅ "In 2026 there are two types of health practices. Most people don't know which one they are." (Specific question: which type am I?)

The more specific the question, the more urgent the click.

### Platform-Specific Formatting Notes
- **Facebook/Instagram:** Text overlay should be 6 words max. First spoken word should be within 0.5 seconds. No silence at the start.
- **YouTube pre-roll:** You have 5 seconds before skip. The hook must deliver the key tension point in 5 words or fewer ON SCREEN before the verbal hook finishes.
- **Reels/TikTok (if applicable):** Pattern interrupt must be visual first — text overlay or action — verbal hook is secondary.

### Pre-VSL Alignment Check
Run this before any hook goes live:
1. State the question the hook plants in one sentence.
2. Open the VSL transcript. Find where that question is answered.
3. Is the answer before or after the 10-minute mark? (Ideally before 7 minutes.)
4. Is the answer MORE satisfying than what the viewer imagined? (It should be.)
5. Does the hook filter OUT consumers and filter IN health experts with practices? Run it by someone outside the organization and ask: "Who do you picture when you hear this?"
